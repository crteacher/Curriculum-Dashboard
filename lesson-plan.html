CA Day 1
§4.3 Completing the Square
Fall 2026
▼
SWBAT Students will be able to solve quadratic equations by completing the square and convert to vertex form.
DO NOW Factor: x² + 6x + 9. What kind of expression is this? ↗ CA Do Now
I Do Introduce completing the square: transform ax² + bx + c into a(x−h)² + k. Walk through x² + 8x + 3 step by step. Connect to vertex form.
We Do Class completes the square for 3 expressions together, narrating each step: "What do I add to make a perfect square trinomial?"
You Do (Together) Partners convert 4 quadratics to vertex form using completing the square and identify the vertex.
You Do (Alone) Practice: CA §4.3 worksheet. ↗ Practice
Technology Desmos: verify vertex form by graphing standard and vertex form side by side.
Assessment Exit ticket: Complete the square for x² − 6x + 2 and state the vertex.
CA Day 2
§4.4 The Quadratic Formula
Fall 2026
▼
SWBAT Students will be able to apply the quadratic formula and use the discriminant to classify solutions.
DO NOW Solve by completing the square: x² + 4x − 5 = 0. ↗ CA Do Now
I Do Derive the quadratic formula by completing the square on ax² + bx + c = 0. Apply to examples with real and complex solutions. Identify the discriminant.
We Do Class solves 3 quadratics with the formula together — one with two real roots, one with a perfect square, one with complex roots.
You Do (Together) Partners solve 4 equations and classify each discriminant (positive, zero, negative) before computing.
You Do (Alone) Practice: CA §4.4 worksheet. ↗ Practice
Technology Desmos: graph the parabola and count x-intercepts — confirm matches discriminant prediction.
Assessment Exit ticket: Use the quadratic formula to solve 2x² − 3x + 5 = 0. What does the discriminant tell you?

C2 Day 1
§1 U-Substitution
Fall 2026
▼
SWBAT Students will be able to evaluate integrals using u-substitution, both indefinite and definite.
DO NOW Differentiate: f(x) = (3x² + 1)⁵. What chain rule step did you use? ↗ Warm-Up
I Do Introduce u-substitution as the reverse chain rule. Walk through ∫2x(x²+1)³ dx: choose u, find du, substitute, integrate, back-substitute.
We Do Class evaluates ∫cos(x)·sin³(x) dx together, voting on u-choice before computing.
You Do (Together) Partners evaluate 4 indefinite integrals using u-sub, then verify by differentiating the result.
You Do (Alone) Practice: C2 §1 worksheet. ↗ Practice
Technology Desmos: check antiderivatives by graphing the derivative of the answer against the original integrand.
Assessment Exit ticket: Evaluate ∫(2x+1)⁴ dx.
C2 Day 2
§2 Advanced U-Substitution
Fall 2026
▼
SWBAT Students will be able to apply u-substitution to complex integrands including trig and exponential forms.
DO NOW Evaluate ∫eˣ dx and ∫sin(x) dx from memory. ↗ Warm-Up
I Do Extend u-substitution to trig and exponential integrands: ∫e^(3x) dx, ∫tan(x) dx. Emphasize recognizing the "inside function" pattern.
We Do Class evaluates ∫sin²(x)cos(x) dx — identify u, confirm du is present, compute.
You Do (Together) Partners work through 4 complex u-sub integrals, checking for proper back-substitution.
You Do (Alone) Practice: C2 §2 worksheet. ↗ Practice
Technology Wolfram Alpha: compare step-by-step solution to your own work.
Assessment Exit ticket: Evaluate ∫xe^(x²) dx.

C2 Day 7
§5 Area Between Curves
Fall 2026
▼
SWBAT Students will be able to compute the area between two curves by integrating the difference of functions.
DO NOW Sketch y = x² and y = x. Which is on top from x = 0 to x = 1? ↗ Warm-Up
I Do Area between curves: A = ∫[a,b](f(x)−g(x))dx. Find intersections, identify top function, compute.
We Do Class sets up and evaluates the area between y = x+2 and y = x² together.
You Do (Together) Partners find the area enclosed by y = 4−x² and y = x+2.
You Do (Alone) Practice: C2 §5 worksheet. ↗ Practice

C2 Day 8
§6 Volumes of Solids
Fall 2026
▼
SWBAT Students will be able to find volumes of solids of revolution using the disk and washer method.
DO NOW What is the area of a circle with radius r? What if r depends on x? ↗ Warm-Up
I Do Disk and washer method: V = π∫[a,b]R²(x)dx and V = π∫[a,b](R²−r²)dx. Work one solid with a hole.
We Do Class sets up the volume of y = √x rotated around the x-axis from 0 to 4.
You Do (Together) Partners set up and evaluate volumes for two different solids of revolution.
You Do (Alone) Practice: C2 §6 worksheet. ↗ Practice
Technology Desmos 3D: visualize solids of revolution.
Assessment Exit ticket: Set up the washer integral for y = x² and y = x rotated around the x-axis.
C2 Day 9
§7 Rotational Solids by Slicing
Fall 2026
▼
SWBAT Students will be able to compute volumes of non-standard solids using cross-sectional slicing.
DO NOW How would you find the volume of a loaf of bread if you could slice it? ↗ Warm-Up
I Do Slicing method for non-standard solids: V = ∫A(x)dx where A(x) is the cross-sectional area. Work examples with square and triangular cross-sections.
We Do Class sets up the volume of a solid with semicircular cross-sections over a given base.
You Do (Together) Partners compute volumes for two solids using the slicing method with different cross-section shapes.
You Do (Alone) Practice: C2 §7 worksheet. ↗ Practice
Technology GeoGebra 3D: sketch solids with known cross-sections.
Assessment Exit ticket: Set up (but do not evaluate) the volume of a solid with square cross-sections perpendicular to the x-axis over [0,2].
