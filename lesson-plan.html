§2Advanced U-Substitution
Fall 2026
▼
SWBATStudents will be able to apply u-substitution to complex integrands including trig and exponential forms
DO NOW↗ Open Calc Do Now
I Do
$\int x\sqrt{x+1}\,dx$ → Let $u=x+1$, $x=u-1$, $dx=du$.$=\int(u-1)u^{1/2}\,du=\int(u^{3/2}-u^{1/2})\,du$$=\dfrac{2u^{5/2}}{5}-\dfrac{2u^{3/2}}{3}+C=\dfrac{2(x+1)^{5/2}}{5}-\dfrac{2(x+1)^{3/2}}{3}+C$
$\int \dfrac{x}{\sqrt{x-3}}\,dx$ → Let $u=x-3$, $x=u+3$.$=\int\dfrac{u+3}{\sqrt{u}}\,du=\int(u^{1/2}+3u^{-1/2})\,du$$=\dfrac{2u^{3/2}}{3}+6u^{1/2}+C$, back-sub $u=x-3$.
We Do
$\int\dfrac{e^x}{1+e^{2x}}\,dx$ → Let $u=e^x$, $du=e^x\,dx$.$=\int\dfrac{du}{1+u^2}=\arctan(e^x)+C$
$\int_0^3 x\sqrt{9-x^2}\,dx$ → Let $u=9-x^2$, $du=-2x\,dx$; limits: $u(0)=9$, $u(3)=0$.$=-\frac{1}{2}\int_9^0\sqrt{u}\,du=\frac{1}{2}\int_0^9 u^{1/2}\,du=\left[\frac{u^{3/2}}{3}\right]_0^9=9$
You Do (Together)
$\int x(x^2+4)^3\,dx$ $\dfrac{(x^2+4)^4}{8}+C$

§5Area Between Curves
Fall 2026
▼
SWBATStudents will be able to compute the area between two curves by integrating the difference of functions
DO NOW↗ Open Calc Do Now
I Do
Find the area between $y=x^2$ and $y=x+2$. → Intersections: $x^2=x+2\Rightarrow x=-1,2$.$A=\int_{-1}^2[(x+2)-x^2]\,dx=\left[\dfrac{x^2}{2}+2x-\dfrac{x^3}{3}\right]_{-1}^2=\dfrac{9}{2}$
Area between $y=4-x^2$ and $y=0$. → $A=\int_{-2}^2(4-x^2)\,dx=\left[4x-\dfrac{x^3}{3}\right]_{-2}^2=\dfrac{32}{3}$
We Do
Area between $y=\sin x$ and $y=\cos x$ on $[0,\pi/2]$. → They cross at $x=\pi/4$.$A=\int_0^{\pi/4}(\cos x-\sin x)\,dx+\int_{\pi/4}^{\pi/2}(\sin x-\cos x)\,dx=2(\sqrt{2}-1)$
Find the area between $x=y^2$ and $x=2-y$. → Intersections: $y^2=2-y\Rightarrow y=-2,1$.$A=\int_{-2}^1[(2-y)-y^2]\,dy=\dfrac{9}{2}$
