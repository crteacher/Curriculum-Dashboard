CA Day 1
§6.2 Simplifying Radicals
Fall 2026
▼
SWBAT Students will be able to simplify radical expressions and convert between radical and rational exponent form.
DO NOW Review: Evaluate 4² and (−3)³. Recall the rule for x^(1/2). ↗ Warm-Up
I Do Model simplifying radical expressions by factoring out perfect squares: √72 → 6√2. Demonstrate converting between radical and rational exponent form: x^(3/2) = x√x.
We Do Class works through 4 mixed radical expressions on the board, discussing factor trees and when to stop simplifying.
You Do (Together) Partners simplify a set of 6 radical expressions, then compare answers and resolve any differences.
You Do (Alone) Practice: CA §6.2 worksheet. ↗ Practice
Technology Desmos: enter radical and rational exponent form side-by-side to verify they are equivalent.
Assessment Exit ticket: Simplify √108. Write x^(2/3) in radical form.
CA Day 2
§6.3 Exponential Functions
Fall 2026
▼
SWBAT Students will be able to evaluate and interpret exponential growth and decay functions.
DO NOW Evaluate: 2⁰, 2⁻¹, 2⁻², 2³. What pattern do you notice? ↗ Warm-Up
I Do Introduce the exponential function f(x) = bˣ for b > 0, b ≠ 1. Evaluate growth (b > 1) vs. decay (0 < b < 1) examples. Contrast with power functions.
We Do Complete an input-output table for f(x) = 2ˣ and g(x) = (1/2)ˣ together, then sketch both graphs.
You Do (Together) Partners classify a list of 8 functions as exponential growth, decay, or neither — justify each classification.
You Do (Alone) Practice: CA §6.3 worksheet. ↗ Practice
Technology Desmos: graph y = aˣ and slide a to see how the base changes the curve.
Assessment Exit ticket: Does f(x) = 3ˣ show growth or decay? What is f(−2)?
CA Day 3
§6.4 Graphing Exponential Functions
Fall 2026
▼
SWBAT Students will be able to graph exponential functions and identify transformations, asymptotes, and intercepts.
DO NOW Describe transformations: how does y = 2ˣ − 3 differ from y = 2ˣ? ↗ Warm-Up
I Do Graph exponential functions with transformations: shifts, reflections, and vertical stretches. Identify the horizontal asymptote and y-intercept for each.
We Do Match 4 exponential graphs to their equations as a class, discussing asymptote and intercept clues.
You Do (Together) Partners sketch two transformed exponential functions from equations and identify key features.
You Do (Alone) Practice: CA §6.4 worksheet. ↗ Practice
Technology Desmos: explore how h, k, and a affect y = a·bˣ⁻ʰ + k using sliders.
Assessment Exit ticket: Identify the asymptote and y-intercept of f(x) = 2ˣ⁺¹ − 4.
CA Day 4
§S.1 Sets and Notation
Fall 2026
▼
SWBAT Students will be able to represent sets using roster and set-builder notation.
DO NOW How would you describe the set of all students in this class? What makes someone a "member"? ↗ Warm-Up
I Do Introduce set notation: roster form {1, 2, 3}, set-builder notation, and interval notation. Define element, subset, empty set, and universal set.
We Do Whole-class activity: write the set of even numbers between 1 and 10 in three different notations.

You Do (Alone) Practice: CA Sets and Notation worksheet. ↗ Practice
Technology None required — notation focus.

CA Day 5
§S.2 Set Operations
Fall 2026
▼
SWBAT Students will be able to perform union, intersection, and complement operations on sets.
DO NOW If A = {1,2,3} and B = {2,3,4}, which elements do they share? ↗ Warm-Up
I Do Define and demonstrate: union (A ∪ B), intersection (A ∩ B), and complement (Aᶜ). Use Venn diagrams to visualize each operation.
We Do Draw a Venn diagram together for two sets of students (athletes vs. honor roll) and label each region.
You Do (Together) Partners solve 6 set operation problems using Venn diagrams, showing all work.
You Do (Alone) Practice: CA Set Operations worksheet. ↗ Practice
Technology Draw.io or paper Venn diagrams.

📙 Calculus 2 · Every Day

C2 Day 1
§1 U-Substitution
Fall 2026
▼
SWBAT Students will be able to evaluate integrals using u-substitution, both indefinite and definite.
DO NOW Differentiate: f(x) = (3x² + 1)⁵. What chain rule step did you use? ↗ Warm-Up
I Do Introduce u-substitution as the "reverse chain rule." Walk through ∫2x(x²+1)³ dx step-by-step: choose u, find du, substitute, integrate, back-substitute.
We Do Class evaluates ∫cos(x)·sin³(x) dx together, voting on u-choice before computing.
You Do (Together) Partners evaluate 4 indefinite integrals using u-sub, then verify by differentiating the result.
You Do (Alone) Practice: C2 §1 worksheet. ↗ Practice
Technology Desmos: check antiderivatives by graphing the derivative of your answer against the original integrand.
Assessment Exit ticket: Evaluate ∫(2x+1)⁴ dx using u-substitution.
C2 Day 2
§2 Advanced U-Substitution
Fall 2026
▼
SWBAT Students will be able to apply u-substitution to complex integrands including trig and exponential forms.
DO NOW Evaluate ∫eˣ dx and ∫sin(x) dx. Recall the derivatives of eˣ and sin(x). ↗ Warm-Up
I Do Extend u-substitution to trig and exponential integrands: ∫e^(3x) dx, ∫tan(x) dx. Emphasize recognizing the "inside function" pattern.
We Do Class evaluates ∫sin²(x)cos(x) dx — identify u, confirm du is present, compute.
You Do (Together) Partners work through 4 complex u-sub integrals, checking for proper back-substitution.
You Do (Alone) Practice: C2 §2 worksheet. ↗ Practice
Technology Wolfram Alpha: compare step-by-step solutions to your own work.
Assessment Exit ticket: Evaluate ∫xe^(x²) dx.
C2 Day 3
§3 Trig Power Reduction
Fall 2026
▼
SWBAT Students will be able to integrate powers of sine and cosine using power-reducing identities.
DO NOW Recall: cos²x + sin²x = 1. Solve for sin²x and cos²x separately. ↗ Warm-Up
I Do Derive power-reducing identities: sin²x = (1−cos2x)/2, cos²x = (1+cos2x)/2. Apply to evaluate ∫sin²x dx and ∫cos⁴x dx.
We Do Class works ∫sin²x·cos²x dx together using both identities sequentially.
You Do (Together) Partners evaluate ∫sin³x dx and ∫cos⁵x dx using a split-one-factor strategy.
You Do (Alone) Practice: C2 §3 worksheet. ↗ Practice
Technology Desmos: plot sin²x and (1−cos2x)/2 on the same graph to confirm the identity.
Assessment Exit ticket: Set up (but do not evaluate) ∫sin⁴x dx using power reduction.
C2 Day 4
§4 Inverse Trig Integrals
Fall 2026
▼
SWBAT Students will be able to evaluate integrals resulting in inverse trigonometric functions.
DO NOW Differentiate: arctan(x). What pattern do you notice with 1/(1+x²)? ↗ Warm-Up
I Do Develop the three inverse trig integral forms: ∫du/√(a²−u²), ∫du/(a²+u²), ∫du/(u√(u²−a²)). Work examples matching to each form.
We Do Class categorizes 4 integrals by which inverse trig form applies before evaluating the first one together.
You Do (Together) Partners evaluate ∫dx/(4+x²) and ∫dx/√(9−x²), labeling a and u clearly.
You Do (Alone) Practice: C2 §4 worksheet. ↗ Practice
Technology Desmos: graph the antiderivative and check with the derivative.
Assessment Exit ticket: Identify the form and evaluate ∫dx/(1+9x²).
C2 Day 5
§5 Area Between Curves
Fall 2026
▼
SWBAT Students will be able to compute the area between two curves by integrating the difference of functions.
DO NOW Sketch y = x² and y = x on the same axes. Which is on top from x=0 to x=1? ↗ Warm-Up
I Do Develop the area-between-curves formula: A = ∫[a,b] (f(x)−g(x)) dx. Work through finding intersection points, identifying top/bottom function, and computing.
We Do Class sets up and evaluates the area between y = x+2 and y = x² together, narrating each step.
You Do (Together) Partners find the area enclosed by y = 4−x² and y = x+2, finding intersections first.
You Do (Alone) Practice: C2 §5 worksheet. ↗ Practice
Technology Desmos: shade the region between two curves and estimate area visually before computing.
Assessment Exit ticket: Set up (but do not evaluate) the integral for the area between y = x² and y = 2x.
