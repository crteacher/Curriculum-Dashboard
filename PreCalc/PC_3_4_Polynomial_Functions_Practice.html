Polynomial Functions

PreCalc  ·  §3.4  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Polynomial functions $f(x) = a_n x^n + \cdots + a_1 x + a_0$, where $a_n \neq 0$.
Degree: the largest exponent $n$.  Leading coefficient: $a_n$.
Polynomials are smooth (no sharp corners) and continuous (no breaks).
Zeros & multiplicity A zero at $x = r$ corresponds to a factor $(x - r)$.
If $(x-r)^m$ divides $f$ but $(x-r)^{m+1}$ does not, then $r$ is a zero of multiplicity $m$.
Total number of zeros (with multiplicity) $\leq$ degree.
Number of turning points $<$ degree.
End behavior For large $|x|$, $f(x)$ behaves like $a_n x^n$ (the leading term).
Even degree, $a_n > 0$: both ends up.   Even, $a_n < 0$: both ends down.
Odd degree, $a_n > 0$: left down, right up.   Odd, $a_n < 0$: left up, right down.
