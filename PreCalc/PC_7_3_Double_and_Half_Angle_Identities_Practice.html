Double and Half Angle Identities

PreCalc  ·  §7.3  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Double angle identities $\sin(2x)=2\sin x\cos x$
$\cos(2x)=\cos^2x-\sin^2x=1-2\sin^2x=2\cos^2x-1$
$\tan(2x)=\dfrac{2\tan x}{1-\tan^2x}$
Half angle identities $\sin\!\dfrac{x}{2}=\pm\sqrt{\dfrac{1-\cos x}{2}}$    $\cos\!\dfrac{x}{2}=\pm\sqrt{\dfrac{1+\cos x}{2}}$
The $\pm$ sign is determined by the quadrant of $\dfrac{x}{2}$.
