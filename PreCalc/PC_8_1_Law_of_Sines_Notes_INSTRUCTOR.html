Law of Sines

Pre-Calculus  ·  §8.1  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  INSTRUCTOR

You know two angles and a side of a triangle — but it's not a right triangle. Can you still find the missing sides? What formula generalizes the right-triangle approach?
The Law of Sines

For any triangle with angles $A,B,C$ and opposite sides $a,b,c$:

$$\dfrac{a}{\sin A}=\dfrac{b}{\sin B}=\dfrac{c}{\sin C}$$

Use when you know: AAS or ASA (two angles + any side).

Recall: $A+B+C=180°$, so knowing two angles gives the third.

The Ambiguous Case (SSA)

Given angle $A$, side $a$ (opposite), side $b$ (adjacent):

  • If $a\geq b$: exactly one triangle.
  • If $a\lt b$ and $a\lt b\sin A$: no triangle.
  • If $a\lt b$ and $a=b\sin A$: exactly one (right triangle).
  • If $b\sin A\lt a\lt b$: two triangles.

Find both solutions when two triangles exist; check $B_2=180°-B_1$ fits.

Worked Examples
Example 1 — AAS
In triangle $ABC$: $A=35°$, $B=65°$, $c=15$. Find $a$ and $b$.
$C=80°$. $a=\dfrac{15\sin35°}{\sin80°}\approx8.73$; $b=\dfrac{15\sin65°}{\sin80°}\approx13.80$.
Example 2 — ASA
In triangle: $A=50°$, $C=70°$, $b=20$. Find $a$ and $c$.
$B=60°$. $a=\dfrac{20\sin50°}{\sin60°}\approx17.68$; $c=\dfrac{20\sin70°}{\sin60°}\approx21.71$.
Example 3 — SSA — two solutions
Solve: $A=38°$, $a=9$, $b=12$.
$\sin B=12\sin38°/9\approx0.820$. $B_1\approx55.1°$ (one triangle), $B_2\approx124.9°$ (second triangle — check $A+B_2=162.9°\lt 180°$ ✓). Both are valid.
Example 4 — Area with Law of Sines
Find the area of a triangle with $A=72°$, $b=10$, $c=14$.
Area $=\frac{1}{2}bc\sin A=\frac{1}{2}(10)(14)\sin72°\approx66.6$ sq units.
Practice Problems
1.
In triangle: $A=42°$, $B=68°$, $a=20$. Find $b$.
$b=20\sin68°/\sin42°\approx27.73$.
2.
In triangle: $A=30°$, $C=110°$, $b=5$. Find $c$.
$B=40°$; $c=5\sin110°/\sin40°\approx7.31$.
3.
$A=25°$, $a=5$, $b=8$. How many triangles?
$\sin B=8\sin25°/5\approx0.676$; $B\approx42.6°$ or $B\approx137.4°$; both valid — two triangles.
4.
Area: $B=58°$, $a=7$, $c=9$.
Area $=\frac{1}{2}(7)(9)\sin58°\approx26.7$.
5.
$A=90°$, $b=6$, $a=10$. Find $B$.
$\sin B=6\cdot1/10=0.6\Rightarrow B\approx36.87°$.
6.
In triangle: $A=55°$, $B=80°$, $c=12$. Find the perimeter.
$C=45°$; $a=12\sin55°/\sin45°\approx13.90$; $b=12\sin80°/\sin45°\approx16.69$. Perimeter $\approx42.6$.
