Piecewise Functions

Pre-Calculus  ·  §1.2 Pt 2  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  INSTRUCTOR

Cell phone plans often charge one rate for the first 500 minutes and a different rate after that. How would you write a single formula that captures this two-rate structure?
Defining Piecewise Functions

A piecewise function uses different formulas on different parts of its domain:

$$f(x)=\begin{cases} x^2 & x<0 \\ 2x+1 & x\geq 0 \end{cases}$$

To evaluate: determine which piece applies, then substitute.

$f(-3)=(-3)^2=9$   (use $x^2$ since $-3<0$)
$f(2)=2(2)+1=5$   (use $2x+1$ since $2\geq 0$)

Graphing and the Absolute Value

Absolute value is a special piecewise function:

$$|x|=\begin{cases} x & x\geq 0 \\ -x & x<0 \end{cases}$$

Graphing tips:
• Use open/closed circles at boundaries to show inclusion.
• Check continuity at each boundary point.
• Identify domain and range from the graph.

Common types: absolute value, step functions (e.g. floor), tax-bracket models.

Worked Examples
Example 1 — Evaluate a piecewise function
Let $f(x)=\begin{cases}3x+1 & x<2\\x^2-1 & x\geq 2\end{cases}$. Find $f(-1)$, $f(2)$, $f(5)$.
$f(-1)=3(-1)+1=-2$; $f(2)=4-1=3$; $f(5)=25-1=24$.
Example 2 — Graph a piecewise function
Sketch $g(x)=\begin{cases}-x+2 & x<0\\x^2 & x\geq 0\end{cases}$. State the domain and range.
Left piece: line $y=-x+2$ for $x<0$, open circle at $(0,2)$. Right piece: parabola $y=x^2$ for $x\geq 0$, closed circle at $(0,0)$. Domain $(-\infty,\infty)$; Range $[0,\infty)$.
Example 3 — Absolute value piecewise
Write $f(x)=|2x-4|$ as a piecewise function and evaluate $f(1)$ and $f(3)$.
$f(x)=\begin{cases}4-2x & x<2\\2x-4 & x\geq 2\end{cases}$. $f(1)=2$; $f(3)=2$.
Example 4 — Real-world piecewise
A parking garage charges $\$3$ for the first hour and $\$1.50$ per hour after that. Write $C(h)$ for $h\geq 0$ hours.
$C(h)=\begin{cases}3 & 01\end{cases}$. E.g. $C(3)=3+3=\$6$.
Practice Problems
1.
Evaluate: $f(x)=\begin{cases}x+4&x\leq 1\\2x&x>1\end{cases}$ at $x=-2, 1, 4$.
$f(-2)=2$; $f(1)=5$; $f(4)=8$.
2.
Evaluate $g(x)=|x-3|$ at $x=0, 3, 7$.
$g(0)=3$; $g(3)=0$; $g(7)=4$.
3.
Write $h(x)=|x+1|$ as a piecewise function.
$h(x)=\begin{cases}x+1&x\geq -1\\-x-1&x<-1\end{cases}$.
4.
A plan costs $\$20$ flat for $\leq 100$ texts, then $\$0.10$ each after. Write $C(n)$ and find $C(150)$.
$C(n)=\begin{cases}20&n\leq100\\20+0.1(n-100)&n>100\end{cases}$; $C(150)=\$25$.
5.
Sketch $f(x)=\begin{cases}1&x<0\\-1&x\geq 0\end{cases}$ and state its range.
Range: $\{-1,1\}$. (Step function with jump at $x=0$.)
6.
Is $f(x)=\begin{cases}2x+1&x<1\\2x+3&x\geq 1\end{cases}$ continuous at $x=1$?
Left limit $=3$, right value $=5$. Not continuous — there is a jump at $x=1$.
