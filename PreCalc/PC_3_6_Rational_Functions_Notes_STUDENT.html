Rational Functions

Pre-Calculus  ·  §3.6  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  STUDENT

The function $f(x)=\dfrac{1}{x}$ has an interesting graph — what happens as $x$ gets close to 0? As $x$ grows very large? Can any rational function just ignore these behaviors?
Domain and Vertical Asymptotes

Rational function: $f(x)=\dfrac{p(x)}{q(x)}$, polynomials $p,q$.

Domain: all reals where $q(x)\neq 0$.

Vertical asymptote at $x=a$ if $q(a)=0$ and $p(a)\neq 0$ (after full cancellation). The graph approaches $\pm\infty$ near $x=a$.

Hole (removable discontinuity) if a common factor cancels from both $p$ and $q$.

Horizontal Asymptotes

Let $n=\deg(p)$, $d=\deg(q)$:

  • $n
  • $n=d$: HA at $y=\dfrac{\text{LC}(p)}{\text{LC}(q)}$
  • $n>d$: no HA (oblique or grows without bound)

Graphing steps: factor, note domain, cancel (note holes), find zeros, VAs, HA, sketch with sign chart.

Worked Examples
Example 1 — Domain of rational function
Find the domain of $f(x)=\dfrac{2x^2-4}{x+5}$.
$x+5\neq 0\Rightarrow x\neq-5$. Domain: $(-\infty,-5)\cup(-5,\infty)$.
Example 2 — Vertical asymptotes
Find VAs of $f(x)=\dfrac{x^2}{x^2+4x-21}$.
$x^2+4x-21=(x+7)(x-3)=0\Rightarrow$ VAs at $x=-7$ and $x=3$.
Example 3 — Horizontal asymptotes
Find HA of (a) $f(x)=\dfrac{x-12}{4x^2+x+1}$ and (b) $g(x)=\dfrac{8x^2-x+2}{4x^2-1}$.
(a) $\deg<\deg$: HA $y=0$. (b) $\deg=\deg$: HA $y=\dfrac{8}{4}=2$.
Example 4 — Hole vs. vertical asymptote
Analyze $h(x)=\dfrac{x^2-4}{x-2}$.
$h(x)=\dfrac{(x-2)(x+2)}{x-2}=x+2$, $x\neq 2$. Hole at $x=2$, $y=4$. No VA.
Practice Problems
1.
Find domain of $g(x)=\dfrac{1}{x^2-4}$.
$x\neq\pm2$; domain all reals except $\pm2$.
2.
Find VAs of $h(x)=\dfrac{x^2}{x^2+1}$.
$x^2+1=0$ has no real solutions — no VAs.
3.
Find HA of $f(x)=\dfrac{3x^4-x^2}{x^3-x^2+1}$.
$\deg(p)=4>\deg(q)=3$: no HA.
4.
Identify hole: $f(x)=\dfrac{x^2-9}{x-3}$.
$(x-3)(x+3)/(x-3)=x+3$; hole at $x=3$, $y=6$.
5.
Find all asymptotes of $f(x)=\dfrac{2x+1}{x-3}$.
VA: $x=3$; HA: $y=2$.
6.
What are the zeros of $f(x)=\dfrac{x^2-1}{x+3}$?
Zeros where numerator $=0$: $x=\pm1$ (both in domain since $-3\neq\pm1$).
