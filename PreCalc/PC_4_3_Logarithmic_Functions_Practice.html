Logarithmic Functions

PreCalc  ·  §4.3  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Logarithms as inverses $\log_b y = x \iff b^x = y$   ($b > 0$, $b \neq 1$, $y > 0$).
Common log: $\log x = \log_{10} x$    Natural log: $\ln x = \log_e x$
Key values: $\log_b 1 = 0$;   $\log_b b = 1$;   $\log_b(b^x) = x$;   $b^{\log_b x} = x$.
Domain of $\log_b(f(x))$: all $x$ where $f(x) > 0$.
Properties of logarithms Product: $\log_b(MN) = \log_b M + \log_b N$
Quotient: $\log_b\!\left(\dfrac{M}{N}\right) = \log_b M - \log_b N$
Power: $\log_b(M^p) = p \log_b M$
Change of base: $\log_b M = \dfrac{\ln M}{\ln b} = \dfrac{\log M}{\log b}$
