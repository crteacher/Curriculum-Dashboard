Graphing Trigonometric Functions

PreCalc  ·  §6.0  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Parameters of $y=A\sin(B(x-C))+D$ Amplitude: $|A|$ — half the height of the wave.
Period: $\dfrac{2\pi}{|B|}$ — length of one complete cycle.
Phase shift: $C$ — horizontal shift (right if $C>0$, left if $C<0$).
Vertical shift / Midline: $D$ — midline at $y=D$.
Same parameters apply to $y=A\cos(B(x-C))+D$.
Key facts Range: $[D-|A|,\;D+|A|]$.
$y=\sin x$: passes through $(0,0)$, period $2\pi$, amp $1$.
$y=\cos x$: passes through $(0,1)$, period $2\pi$, amp $1$.
To find $B$ from period $T$: $B=\dfrac{2\pi}{T}$.
Practice Problems

Integers for amplitude, midline, max/min. Period and phase shift: write π as pi (e.g. pi/2, 2pi/3). Negative phase shift: e.g. -pi/4. Press Check or hit Enter.
