Right Triangle Applications

Pre-Calculus  ·  §5.4  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  INSTRUCTOR

A surveyor stands 100 meters from a building and looks up at an angle of $65°$. Without climbing the building, can they find its height?
Angles of Elevation and Depression

Angle of elevation: angle measured upward from horizontal to the line of sight.

Angle of depression: angle measured downward from horizontal to the line of sight.

Both are always measured from the horizontal, never from the vertical.

Setup: draw a right triangle, label what you know, write a trig ratio, solve.

Solving Right Triangles

Solving a right triangle means finding all missing sides and angles.

Given angle $A$ and one side, use SOH-CAH-TOA for the others.
Use the inverse trig ($\sin^{-1}$, $\cos^{-1}$, $\tan^{-1}$) to find angles.
Check: $A+B=90°$ (the two non-right angles), and $a^2+b^2=c^2$ (Pythagorean).

Worked Examples
Example 1 — Angle of elevation
A surveyor is 100 m from a building. The angle of elevation to the top is $65°$. Find the height.
$h=100\tan65°\approx214.5$ m.
Example 2 — Angle of depression
From a cliff 200 m high, a boat is seen at an angle of depression of $15°$. Find the horizontal distance.
$d=\dfrac{200}{\tan15°}\approx746$ m.
Example 3 — Solve the right triangle
A right triangle has angle $A=40°$ and hypotenuse $c=15$. Find sides $a$ and $b$.
$a=15\sin40°\approx9.64$; $b=15\cos40°\approx11.49$.
Example 4 — Bearing/navigation
A plane flies due east 50 km, then due north 80 km. Find the distance from start and the bearing angle.
$d=\sqrt{50^2+80^2}=\sqrt{8900}\approx94.3$ km. Angle from east: $\arctan(80/50)\approx58°$ (bearing N58°W from start reversed).
Practice Problems
1.
A kite string makes $55°$ with the ground and is 100 m long. How high is the kite?
$100\sin55°\approx81.9$ m.
2.
From a 150 m tower, the angle of depression to a car is $25°$. Find distance.
$d=150/\tan25°\approx321.7$ m.
3.
Solve: right triangle with $B=32°$, $a=7$ (opposite to $A=58°$). Find $c$.
$c=7/\sin58°\approx8.25$.
4.
A shadow 12 m long is cast by a 9 m pole. Find the sun's angle of elevation.
$\arctan(9/12)=36.87°\approx37°$.
5.
Convert $150°$ to radians and find $\sin150°$.
$\dfrac{5\pi}{6}$; $\sin150°=\dfrac{1}{2}$.
6.
A right triangle has legs 7 and 24. Find the angles (to nearest degree).
$\arctan(7/24)\approx16°$ and $90°-16°=74°$.
