Exponential Functions

PreCalc  ·  §4.1  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Exponential functions $f(x) = a \cdot b^x$, where $a > 0$, $b > 0$, $b \neq 1$.
$a$ = initial value (y-intercept);   $b$ = base (growth factor).
$b > 1$: growth (increasing).   $0 < b < 1$: decay (decreasing).
Key features: continuous, passes through $(0,a)$, horizontal asymptote at $y = 0$, always positive.
Laws of exponents & compound interest $b^x \cdot b^y = b^{x+y}$    $\dfrac{b^x}{b^y} = b^{x-y}$    $(b^x)^y = b^{xy}$
Compound interest: $A = P\!\left(1 + \dfrac{r}{n}\right)^{nt}$   ($P$ = principal, $r$ = annual rate, $n$ = times/year, $t$ = years)
Continuous compounding: $A = Pe^{rt}$
