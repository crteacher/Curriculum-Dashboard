Inverse Trigonometric Functions

PreCalc  ·  §6.4  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Inverse trig definitions $y=\arcsin x$: domain $[-1,1]$, range $\left[-\dfrac{\pi}{2},\dfrac{\pi}{2}\right]$ (QI and QIV).
$y=\arccos x$: domain $[-1,1]$, range $[0,\pi]$ (QI and QII).
$y=\arctan x$: domain $(-\infty,\infty)$, range $\left(-\dfrac{\pi}{2},\dfrac{\pi}{2}\right)$ (QI and QIV).
Notation: $\arcsin x = \sin^{-1}x$ (the $^{-1}$ means inverse, not reciprocal).
Evaluating and composing $\sin(\arcsin x)=x$ for $x\in[-1,1]$;   $\arcsin(\sin\theta)=\theta$ only if $\theta\in[-\pi/2,\pi/2]$.
For $\arcsin(\sin\theta)$ outside range: find the reference angle and adjust sign.
Composition trick: to find $\cos(\arcsin\tfrac{3}{5})$, draw a right triangle with opp $=3$, hyp $=5$, adj $=4$; then read off $\cos=\tfrac{4}{5}$.
Practice Problems

Radian answers: write π as pi (e.g. pi/6, -pi/4, 2pi/3). Fraction answers: e.g. 4/5 or 12/13. Decimal answers: enter as-is (e.g. 0.4). Press Check or hit Enter.
