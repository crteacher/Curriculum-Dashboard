Graphing Sine and Cosine — Extended

PreCalc  ·  §6.1  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Graphing strategy Given $y=A\sin(Bx+C)+D$: factor first to $A\sin(B(x-C/B))+D$.
Phase shift $= -C/B$ (right if positive). Period $= 2\pi/|B|$. Midline $y=D$.
Five key points for one period of sine: $\;0,\;T/4,\;T/2,\;3T/4,\;T$ (start, max, mid, min, end).
Cosine starts at a max (or min if $A<0$), sine starts at the midline.
Reading graphs Given a graph: $\text{Midline} = \dfrac{\text{max}+\text{min}}{2}$   $\text{Amplitude} = \dfrac{\text{max}-\text{min}}{2}$
Period = horizontal distance between two consecutive identical points.
Identify sin vs cos: starts at midline $\to$ sine; starts at max/min $\to$ cosine.
Practice Problems

Integers for amplitude, midline, max. Phase shift and B values involving π: write π as pi (e.g. pi/2, -pi/4, 2pi/3). y-intercept: enter the y-value or (0,value). Press Check or hit Enter.
