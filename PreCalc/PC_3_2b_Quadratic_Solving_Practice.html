Quadratic Functions — Solving

PreCalc  ·  §3.2 Part 2  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Completing the square To convert $x^2+bx+c$ to vertex form $(x+b/2)^2 + k$:
1.   Take half the middle coefficient: $h = -b/2$.
2.   Subtract $(b/2)^2$ to compensate: $k = c - (b/2)^2$.
Result: $(x-h)^2 + k$ with vertex at $(h,\,k)$.
Works with $a\neq 1$ too — factor out $a$ first.
Quadratic formula & discriminant For $ax^2+bx+c=0$:   $x = \dfrac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}$
Discriminant $\Delta = b^2-4ac$:
• $\Delta > 0$: two distinct real roots.  • $\Delta = 0$: one repeated real root.
• $\Delta < 0$: two complex (non-real) roots — of the form $p \pm qi$.
Note: complex roots always appear as conjugate pairs.
