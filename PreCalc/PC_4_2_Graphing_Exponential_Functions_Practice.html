Graphing Exponential Functions

PreCalc  ·  §4.2  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Transformations of exponential graphs Starting from $y = b^x$ (HA: $y=0$, passes through $(0,1)$):
$y = b^x + k$:   shift up $k$ units; new HA at $y = k$.
$y = b^{x-h}$:   shift right $h$ units (left if $h < 0$).
$y = -b^x$:   reflect over the $x$-axis; HA still $y = 0$.
$y = a \cdot b^x$:   vertical stretch/shrink by $|a|$; $y$-intercept becomes $(0,a)$.
Finding an exponential model from data Given $f(t) = a \cdot b^t$ and two data points $(t_1, y_1)$, $(t_2, y_2)$:
$b = \left(\dfrac{y_2}{y_1}\right)^{1/(t_2 - t_1)}$    and    $a = \dfrac{y_1}{b^{t_1}}$
Range of $a \cdot b^x + k$:   $(k, \infty)$ if $a > 0$;   $(-\infty, k)$ if $a < 0$.
