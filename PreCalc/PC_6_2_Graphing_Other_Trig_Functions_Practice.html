Graphing Other Trig Functions

PreCalc  ·  §6.2  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Tangent and cotangent $y=\tan x$: period $\pi$; VAs at $x=\tfrac{\pi}{2}+n\pi$; passes through $(0,0)$; increasing.
$y=\cot x$: period $\pi$; VAs at $x=n\pi$; passes through $(\tfrac{\pi}{2},0)$.
For $y=A\tan(B(x-C))+D$: period $=\dfrac{\pi}{|B|}$.
For $y=A\cot(B(x-C))+D$: period $=\dfrac{\pi}{|B|}$.
Secant and cosecant $y=\sec x=\dfrac{1}{\cos x}$: VAs where $\cos x=0$; period $2\pi$; range $(-\infty,-1]\cup[1,\infty)$.
$y=\csc x=\dfrac{1}{\sin x}$: VAs where $\sin x=0$; period $2\pi$; same range.
Graph sec/csc by drawing the parent cos/sin first, then sketching U-curves at its peaks.
Practice Problems

Period and VA locations: write π as pi (e.g. pi/3, pi/2). Behavior: increasing or decreasing. Integers for range boundary values. Press Check or hit Enter.
