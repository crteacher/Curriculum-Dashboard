Parametric Equations

PreCalc  ·  §3.3  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Parametric equations Instead of $y$ as a function of $x$, both are expressed as functions of a third variable $t$ (often time):
$x = f(t)$,   $y = g(t)$.
To eliminate the parameter: solve one equation for $t$, substitute into the other to get $y$ in terms of $x$.
Or use a direct relationship if one variable is already linear in $t$.
Parabolic motion (feet & seconds) A projectile launched with horizontal velocity $v_x$ and initial vertical velocity $v_y$ from height $h_0$:
$x(t) = v_x \cdot t$    (horizontal position)
$y(t) = -16t^2 + v_y t + h_0$    (vertical position; gravity $\approx 32$ ft/s²)
Max height: at $t^* = \dfrac{v_y}{32}$,   height $= y(t^*)$.
Landing: set $y(t) = 0$ and solve for positive $t$.
