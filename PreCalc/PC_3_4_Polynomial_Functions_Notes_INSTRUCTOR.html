Polynomial Functions

Pre-Calculus  ·  §3.4  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  INSTRUCTOR

A linear function has one "bend" (none, actually) and a quadratic has one. How many times can a degree-$n$ polynomial bend, and how does the leading term control the ends of the graph?
Polynomials, Degree, and Zeros

Polynomial: $f(x)=a_nx^n+\cdots+a_1x+a_0$, $a_n\neq0$.

Degree: largest power $n$. Leading coefficient: $a_n$.

Zeros: $x=r$ is a zero if $f(r)=0$, i.e. $(x-r)$ is a factor.

Multiplicity: if $(x-r)^m$ is a factor, $r$ has multiplicity $m$.

  • Odd multiplicity: graph crosses $x$-axis at $r$.
  • Even multiplicity: graph touches (bounces off) $x$-axis at $r$.
▸ Show Proof

Near a zero $r$, factor the polynomial as $f(x)=(x-r)^m\,g(x)$, where $g$ holds all the other factors and $g(r)\neq 0$ — if it were zero, $r$ would have a higher multiplicity. Since $g$ is continuous and nonzero at $r$, it keeps a constant sign for every $x$ close enough to $r$. So near $r$, the sign of $f(x)$ is controlled entirely by the factor $(x-r)^m$.

Watch $(x-r)$ as $x$ passes through $r$: it is negative just left of $r$ and positive just right. Raising to the power $m$ decides what happens to that sign — a negative number to an even power becomes positive, but to an odd power stays negative.

Even $m$: $(x-r)^m\ge 0$ on both sides, so $f$ keeps the same sign across $r$ — the graph drops to the axis and turns back (touches/bounces).

Odd $m$: $(x-r)^m$ flips from negative to positive, so $f$ changes sign — the graph passes straight through (crosses).

∎

End Behavior

End Behavior Theorem: for large $|x|$, $f(x)\approx a_nx^n$.

$a_n>0$, even $n$$\uparrow\uparrow$ (both ends up)
$a_n<0$, even $n$$\downarrow\downarrow$ (both ends down)
$a_n>0$, odd $n$$\downarrow\uparrow$ (left down, right up)
$a_n<0$, odd $n$$\uparrow\downarrow$ (left up, right down)
▸ Show Proof

Why does the single leading term take over when $|x|$ is large? Factor $a_nx^n$ out of every term:

$$f(x)=a_nx^n\left(1+\frac{a_{n-1}}{a_n}\cdot\frac{1}{x}+\cdots+\frac{a_0}{a_n}\cdot\frac{1}{x^n}\right).$$

As $|x|$ grows, each piece $\dfrac{1}{x},\dfrac{1}{x^2},\dots$ shrinks toward $0$, so everything inside the parentheses collapses to $1$. That leaves $f(x)\approx a_nx^n$ — the leading term alone sets how the graph behaves far out.

From there the four cases follow: $x^n$ is positive on both ends when $n$ is even (both ends go the same way), while for odd $n$ it keeps the sign of $x$ (ends go opposite ways). The sign of $a_n$ then flips the whole picture up or down.

∎

A degree-$n$ poly has at most $n$ real zeros and at most $n-1$ turning points.

Worked Examples
Example 1 — Identify polynomial and degree
Which are polynomials? (a) $3x^4-2x+5$ (b) $\sqrt{x}+1$ (c) $x^{-2}+1$ (d) $x^3+x^2-7$
(a) Yes, degree 4. (b) No — $\sqrt{x}=x^{1/2}$, not integer exponent. (c) No — negative exponent. (d) Yes, degree 3.
Example 2 — Zeros and multiplicities
Find zeros and multiplicities of $f(x)=5(x-2)(x+3)^2(x-\tfrac{1}{2})^4$.
Zero $x=2$ (mult 1, crosses); $x=-3$ (mult 2, bounces); $x=\tfrac{1}{2}$ (mult 4, bounces).
Example 3 — End behavior
Describe end behavior of $f(x)=-3x^5+7x^2-1$.
$a_n=-3<0$, odd degree $5$: as $x\to-\infty$, $f\to+\infty$; as $x\to+\infty$, $f\to-\infty$.
Example 4 — Build a polynomial
Find a degree-3 polynomial with zeros $-3, 2, 5$ and leading coefficient $1$.
$f(x)=(x+3)(x-2)(x-5)=x^3-4x^2-11x+30$.
Practice Problems
1.
State degree and leading coefficient of $f(x)=-7x^6+3x^2-x$.
Degree 6, leading coefficient $-7$.
2.
Find zeros of $f(x)=(x+1)^2(x-3)$.
$x=-1$ (mult 2), $x=3$ (mult 1).
3.
What is end behavior of $f(x)=4x^3-2x$?
$a_n=4>0$, odd degree: $\downarrow\uparrow$.
4.
How many turning points can $f(x)=x^4-3x^2+1$ have?
At most $4-1=3$.
5.
Write a degree-2 poly with zeros $x=1$ and $x=-4$ and leading coefficient $3$.
$f(x)=3(x-1)(x+4)=3x^2+9x-12$.
6.
At a zero of multiplicity 3, does the graph cross or bounce?
Odd multiplicity — the graph crosses the $x$-axis.
