Triangle Definition of Trig Functions

PreCalc  ·  §5.3b  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
SOH-CAH-TOA For angle $\theta$ in a right triangle with opposite $o$, adjacent $a$, hypotenuse $h$:
$\sin\theta=\dfrac{o}{h}$   $\cos\theta=\dfrac{a}{h}$   $\tan\theta=\dfrac{o}{a}$    $\csc\theta=\dfrac{h}{o}$   $\sec\theta=\dfrac{h}{a}$   $\cot\theta=\dfrac{a}{o}$
Special triangles 45-45-90 (sides $1,1,\sqrt{2}$): $\sin 45°=\cos 45°=\dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$, $\tan 45°=1$
30-60-90 (sides $1,\sqrt{3},2$): $\sin 30°=\cos 60°=\dfrac{1}{2}$; $\cos 30°=\sin 60°=\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$; $\tan 30°=\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{3}$; $\tan 60°=\sqrt{3}$
Pythagorean triples: $3$-$4$-$5$; $5$-$12$-$13$; $8$-$15$-$17$
