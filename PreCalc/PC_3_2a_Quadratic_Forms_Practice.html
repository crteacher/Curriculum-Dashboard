Quadratic Functions — Forms

PreCalc  ·  §3.2 Part 1  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Three forms of a quadratic Standard: $f(x)=ax^2+bx+c$  — reads off y-intercept $c$ and tells direction (sign of $a$).
Vertex: $f(x)=a(x-h)^2+k$  — vertex at $(h,k)$; max/min value is $k$.
Factored: $f(x)=a(x-r_1)(x-r_2)$  — zeros at $x=r_1$ and $x=r_2$.
The leading coefficient $a$ is the same in all three forms.
Key features Opens up if $a>0$ (minimum);  opens down if $a<0$ (maximum).
Vertex x-coordinate: $x = -\dfrac{b}{2a}$ (from standard form).
y-intercept: always $(0,\,c)$ — just set $x=0$.
Zeros/x-intercepts: read from factored form, or solve $f(x)=0$.
Practice Problems

Enter the vertex as (h,k), zeros as r1,r2, intercepts as (0,c), or up/down for orientation. Press Check or hit Enter.
