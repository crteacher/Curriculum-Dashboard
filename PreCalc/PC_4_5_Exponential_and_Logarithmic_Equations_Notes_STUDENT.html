Exponential and Logarithmic Equations

Pre-Calculus  ·  §4.5  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  STUDENT

To solve $5^x=200$, you can't easily write 200 as a power of 5. What tool lets you pull the exponent down and solve for $x$?
Solving Exponential Equations

Strategy 1: if both sides can be written with the same base, set exponents equal.
E.g. $2^{3x}=8^{x-1}\Rightarrow 2^{3x}=2^{3(x-1)}\Rightarrow 3x=3x-3$ — no solution.

Strategy 2: take $\ln$ (or $\log$) of both sides:
$b^x=c\Rightarrow x\ln b=\ln c\Rightarrow x=\dfrac{\ln c}{\ln b}$.

Solving Logarithmic Equations

Strategy 1: condense to a single log, then convert to exponential form.
$\log_b(f(x))=c\Rightarrow f(x)=b^c$.

Strategy 2: if $\log_b A=\log_b B$, then $A=B$.

Always check: the argument of each log must be $>0$ in the original equation (reject extraneous solutions).

Worked Examples
Example 1 — Solve exponential
Solve $5^x=200$.
$x=\dfrac{\ln200}{\ln5}=\dfrac{5.298}{1.609}\approx3.29$.
Example 2 — Solve exponential — same base
Solve $4^{2x-1}=8$.
$2^{4x-2}=2^3\Rightarrow 4x-2=3\Rightarrow x=\tfrac{5}{4}$.
Example 3 — Solve logarithmic
Solve $\log_2(3x-1)=4$.
$3x-1=2^4=16\Rightarrow x=\tfrac{17}{3}$.
Example 4 — Solve with properties
Solve $\log x+\log(x-3)=1$.
$\log[x(x-3)]=1\Rightarrow x^2-3x=10\Rightarrow x^2-3x-10=0\Rightarrow(x-5)(x+2)=0$. Reject $x=-2$ (log undefined). $x=5$.
Practice Problems
1.
Solve $3^x=81$.
$x=4$.
2.
Solve $e^{2x}=7$.
$x=\dfrac{\ln7}{2}\approx0.973$.
3.
Solve $\log(x+2)=3$.
$x+2=1000\Rightarrow x=998$.
4.
Solve $2\ln x=\ln 9$.
$\ln x^2=\ln9\Rightarrow x^2=9\Rightarrow x=3$ (reject $x=-3$).
5.
Solve $5^{x+1}=25^{x-1}$.
$5^{x+1}=5^{2(x-1)}\Rightarrow x+1=2x-2\Rightarrow x=3$.
6.
Solve $\log_3(x^2-4)=2$.
$x^2-4=9\Rightarrow x^2=13\Rightarrow x=\pm\sqrt{13}$ (check both: both valid).
