Polar Coordinates

PreCalc  ·  §8.3  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Polar coordinates $(r,\theta)$ $r$ = distance from origin; $\theta$ = angle in standard position.
Polar $\to$ Cartesian: $x=r\cos\theta$,   $y=r\sin\theta$
Cartesian $\to$ Polar: $r=\sqrt{x^2+y^2}$,   $\theta=\arctan\!\left(\dfrac{y}{x}\right)$ (adjust for quadrant)
Key facts The same point can be written as $(r,\theta)$ or $(-r,\theta+\pi)$.
Also, adding any multiple of $2\pi$ to $\theta$ gives the same point.
$r\cos\theta=x$   and   $r\sin\theta=y$ are the conversion identities.
Practice Problems

Integer answers: e.g. 3. Square-root answers: sqrt(3), 2sqrt(3). Angle answers: write π as pi (e.g. pi/4, 5pi/6). Negative: -6. Press Check or hit Enter.
