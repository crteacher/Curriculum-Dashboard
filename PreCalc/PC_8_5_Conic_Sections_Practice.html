Conic Sections

PreCalc  ·  §8.5  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Parabola & Ellipse (centered at origin) Parabola: $x^2=4py$ (opens up/down, focus $(0,p)$)   or   $y^2=4px$ (opens right/left, focus $(p,0)$)
Ellipse: $\dfrac{x^2}{a^2}+\dfrac{y^2}{b^2}=1$,   $c^2=a^2-b^2$,   foci $(\pm c,0)$ when $a>b$
Hyperbola & Circle Hyperbola: $\dfrac{x^2}{a^2}-\dfrac{y^2}{b^2}=1$,   $c^2=a^2+b^2$,   asymptotes $y=\pm\dfrac{b}{a}x$
Circle: $x^2+y^2=r^2$, radius $r$.
One squared term only $\Rightarrow$ parabola. Both squared, same sign $\Rightarrow$ circle/ellipse. Opposite signs $\Rightarrow$ hyperbola.
