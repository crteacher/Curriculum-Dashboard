Angles and Their Measure

PreCalc  ·  §5.1  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Degrees and radians One full rotation $= 360° = 2\pi$ rad.   Key equivalence: $180° = \pi$ rad.
Deg $\to$ rad: multiply by $\dfrac{\pi}{180}$.   Rad $\to$ deg: multiply by $\dfrac{180}{\pi}$.
$30°=\dfrac{\pi}{6}$;  $45°=\dfrac{\pi}{4}$;  $60°=\dfrac{\pi}{3}$;  $90°=\dfrac{\pi}{2}$;  $120°=\dfrac{2\pi}{3}$;  $270°=\dfrac{3\pi}{2}$
Arc length, coterminal angles, speed Arc length: $s = r\theta$   ($\theta$ must be in radians).
Coterminal angles share the same terminal side — add or subtract $2\pi$ (or $360°$).
Reference angle: smallest positive acute angle to the nearest $x$-axis.
Angular speed: $\omega = \theta/t$.   Linear speed: $v = r\omega$.
