Triangles with Inverse Trig

PreCalc  ·  §6.5  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Right triangles and inverse trig To find a missing angle $\theta$ in a right triangle:
$\theta=\arcsin\!\left(\dfrac{\text{opp}}{\text{hyp}}\right)$   $\theta=\arccos\!\left(\dfrac{\text{adj}}{\text{hyp}}\right)$   $\theta=\arctan\!\left(\dfrac{\text{opp}}{\text{adj}}\right)$
For exact answers, recognize special ratios: $\arcsin\tfrac{1}{2}=30°$, $\arctan 1=45°$, $\arccos\tfrac{1}{2}=60°$.
Applications Height/distance: set up tan(elevation) = height / horizontal distance.
Two-angle problems: write two tan equations, solve simultaneously.
Composition: $\sin(\arctan\tfrac{3}{4})$ — draw a 3-4-5 triangle, read sin $=\tfrac{3}{5}$.
Both angles of a right triangle sum to 90°: $\theta_1+\theta_2=90°$.
Practice Problems

Degree answers: plain integers (e.g. 30, -45). Fraction answers: 3/5 or 12/13. Square root answers: sqrt(3)/2 or sqrt(3). Press Check or hit Enter.
