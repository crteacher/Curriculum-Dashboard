Inverse Functions

PreCalc  ·  §1.7  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
One-to-one & inverses A function has an inverse if and only if it is one-to-one — passes the Horizontal Line Test.
$f^{-1}$ reverses $f$:   $f^{-1}(f(x))=x$   and   $f(f^{-1}(x))=x$.
Domain of $f^{-1}$ = Range of $f$;   graph of $f^{-1}$ reflects over $y=x$.
Finding $f^{-1}$: swap & solve Write $y=f(x)$, swap $x \leftrightarrow y$, then solve for $y$.
Example: $f(x)=3x-6$  →  $x=3y-6$  →  $y=\dfrac{x+6}{3}$  →  $f^{-1}(x)=\dfrac{x+6}{3}$.
Check: $f(f^{-1}(x))=3\cdot\dfrac{x+6}{3}-6=x$ ✓
