Linear Models from Data

PreCalc  ·  §2.4  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Correlation coefficient r $r$ measures the strength and direction of a linear relationship. Always $-1 \leq r \leq 1$.
• $r > 0$: positive correlation (y tends to increase with x).
• $r < 0$: negative correlation (y tends to decrease as x increases).
• $|r|$ near 1 → strong;   $|r|$ near 0 → weak.
Rule of thumb: $|r| > 0.7$ = strong,   $0.3 < |r| < 0.7$ = moderate,   $|r| < 0.3$ = weak.
Least-squares regression line The regression line $\hat{y} = ax + b$ minimizes the sum of squared residuals.
$a$ = slope (rate of change of predicted y per unit x).
$b$ = y-intercept (predicted y when $x=0$).
Plug in an x-value to predict the corresponding $\hat{y}$.
