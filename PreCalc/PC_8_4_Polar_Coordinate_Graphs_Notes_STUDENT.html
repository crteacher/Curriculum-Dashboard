Polar Coordinate Graphs

Pre-Calculus  ·  §8.4  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  STUDENT

In rectangular coordinates, $y=\sin x$ makes a wave. In polar coordinates, $r=\sin\theta$ makes a circle. Why the difference — and what shapes do other polar equations make?
Common Polar Curves

Circles: $r=a$ (centered at origin); $r=2a\cos\theta$ or $r=2a\sin\theta$ (pass through origin).

Limaçons: $r=a\pm b\cos\theta$ or $r=a\pm b\sin\theta$

Cardioid (special limaçon): $r=a(1\pm\cos\theta)$, $a=b$.

Rose curves: $r=a\cos(n\theta)$ — $n$ petals if $n$ odd; $2n$ petals if $n$ even.

Lemniscate: $r^2=a^2\cos(2\theta)$.

Graphing Strategy

To graph polar equations:

  1. Make a table of $\theta$ and $r$ values.
  2. Plot $(r,\theta)$ points.
  3. Connect smoothly.

Key $\theta$ values: $0,\pi/6,\pi/4,\pi/3,\pi/2,2\pi/3,3\pi/4,5\pi/6,\pi,\ldots$

Identify symmetry: axis, pole, line $\theta=\pi/2$.

Worked Examples
Example 1 — Graph a circle
Identify and graph $r=3\cos\theta$.
$r^2=3r\cos\theta\Rightarrow x^2+y^2=3x$: circle centered at $(3/2,0)$, radius $3/2$.
Example 2 — Cardioid
Make a table and sketch $r=1+\cos\theta$.
At $\theta=0$: $r=2$; $\pi/2$: $r=1$; $\pi$: $r=0$; $3\pi/2$: $r=1$. Heart shape through origin.
Example 3 — Rose curve
How many petals does $r=4\cos(3\theta)$ have? Sketch.
$n=3$ (odd): 3 petals. Max $r=4$ at $\theta=0,2\pi/3,4\pi/3$.

Practice Problems
1.
Identify $r=5$.
Circle of radius 5 centered at origin.
2.
Sketch $r=2(1-\sin\theta)$ — classify.
Cardioid ($a=b=2$).
3.
How many petals: $r=\cos(4\theta)$?
$n=4$ even: $2\times4=8$ petals.
4.
Convert $r=4\sin\theta$ to rectangular.
$x^2+(y-2)^2=4$ — circle center $(0,2)$, radius 2.
5.
At what $\theta$ does $r=1+2\cos\theta$ equal zero?
$\cos\theta=-1/2\Rightarrow\theta=2\pi/3,4\pi/3$.
6.
Describe symmetry of $r=3\cos(2\theta)$.
Symmetric about both axes and the pole.
