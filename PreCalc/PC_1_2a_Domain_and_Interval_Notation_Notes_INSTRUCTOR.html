Interval and Set-Builder Notation

Interval notation uses brackets $[\,,\,]$ (includes endpoint) and parentheses $(\,,\,)$ (excludes endpoint).

Examples:
$x>1$: $(1,\infty)$
$x\leq 1$: $(-\infty,1]$
$3$x\neq 0$: $(-\infty,0)\cup(0,\infty)$

Set-builder: $\{x\mid x>1\}$, $\{x\mid x\neq 0\}$, etc.

Finding the Domain

Most functions have domain $(-\infty,\infty)$. Restrictions come from:

  1. Denominators: exclude zeros of the denominator.
  2. Even roots: set the radicand $\geq 0$ and solve.
  3. Logarithms: argument must be $>0$ (covered later).

Find domain before simplifying!

Strategy: identify the problem type, write the restriction, solve, then express in interval notation.

Worked Examples
Example 1 — Denominator restriction
Find the domain of $f(x)=\dfrac{2x+1}{x^2-9}$.
$x^2-9=0\Rightarrow x=\pm3$. Domain: $(-\infty,-3)\cup(-3,3)\cup(3,\infty)$.
Example 2 — Even-root restriction
Find the domain of $g(x)=\sqrt{2x-6}$.
$2x-6\geq 0\Rightarrow x\geq 3$. Domain: $[3,\infty)$.
Example 3 — Combined restriction
Find the domain of $h(x)=\dfrac{\sqrt{x+4}}{x-1}$.
Need $x+4\geq 0$ (so $x\geq -4$) AND $x\neq 1$. Domain: $[-4,1)\cup(1,\infty)$.
Example 4 — Interval notation practice
Write $-2\leq x<5$ and $x\leq -1$ or $x>4$ in interval notation.
$[-2,5)$ and $(-\infty,-1]\cup(4,\infty)$.
