Graph Properties

Pre-Calculus  ·  §1.3  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  INSTRUCTOR

If you graph your distance from home over a day, what does it mean when the graph goes up? Down? Stays flat? How do the highest and lowest points relate to your travel?
Increasing, Decreasing, Constant

Reading graphs left to right:

  • Increasing on $(a,b)$: $a
  • Decreasing on $(a,b)$: $af(x_2)$
  • Constant on $(a,b)$: $f(x_1)=f(x_2)$ for all $x_1,x_2$

Report intervals using $x$-values, using open intervals.

▸ Common Mistake

Increasing and decreasing intervals are reported with $x$-values, not $y$-values. Students see the curve rise from height $2$ to height $5$ and write "increasing on $(2,5)$" — but those are outputs.

The interval names the $x$-positions over which the curve rises. Read the horizontal axis, and use open intervals at the turning points.

Maxima, Minima & Average Rate of Change

Local max/min: highest/lowest in some open interval around the point.

Absolute max/min: highest/lowest on the entire domain (may not exist).

Average rate of change from $a$ to $b$:

$$\text{ARC}=\dfrac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}$$

This equals the slope of the secant line connecting $(a,f(a))$ and $(b,f(b))$.

▸ Common Mistake

Average rate of change keeps the same order on top and bottom: $\dfrac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}$. Students flip one of them — writing $\dfrac{f(b)-f(a)}{a-b}$ — which reverses the sign, or they invert the whole thing to $\dfrac{b-a}{f(b)-f(a)}$.

It's a slope: change in output over change in input, with both differences taken in the same direction ($b$ then $a$).

Worked Examples
Example 1 — Increasing/decreasing from graph
A function increases on $(-\infty,1)$, decreases on $(1,3)$, and increases again on $(3,\infty)$. Identify local max and min.
Local max at $x=1$; local min at $x=3$.
Example 2 — Average rate of change
Find the ARC of $f(x)=3x^2$ from $x=1$ to $x=3$.
$\dfrac{f(3)-f(1)}{3-1}=\dfrac{27-3}{2}=\dfrac{24}{2}=12$.
Example 3 — ARC from a table
Table: $x$: 0,2,4,6; $f(x)$: 1,5,3,7. Find the ARC from $x=2$ to $x=6$.
$\dfrac{7-5}{6-2}=\dfrac{2}{4}=0.5$.
Example 4 — Identify properties from formula
For $f(x)=-x^2+4$, find any absolute max or min, and state where $f$ is increasing and decreasing.
Vertex at $(0,4)$ — absolute max of 4. Increasing on $(-\infty,0)$, decreasing on $(0,\infty)$.
Practice Problems
1.
A graph increases on $(-2,1)$, has a local max of 5 at $x=1$, then decreases. Describe the function's behavior.
Increasing on $(-2,1)$, local max $=5$ at $x=1$, decreasing after.
2.
Find the ARC of $f(x)=x^2+1$ from $x=0$ to $x=4$.
$\dfrac{17-1}{4}=4$.
3.
Find the ARC of $f(x)=2x-3$ from $x=1$ to $x=5$.
$\dfrac{7-(-1)}{4}=2$ (equals slope of line).
4.
A function has an absolute min of $-2$ but no absolute max. Sketch a possible graph.
E.g. $y=x^2-2$, opening upward with vertex at minimum.
5.
On what interval(s) is $f(x)=|x|$ decreasing?
Decreasing on $(-\infty,0)$.
6.
Find the ARC of $g(x)=x^3$ from $x=-1$ to $x=2$.
$\dfrac{8-(-1)}{3}=3$.
