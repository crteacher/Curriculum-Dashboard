Complex Numbers

PreCalc  ·  §3.1  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Imaginary unit & powers of i $i = \sqrt{-1}$, so $i^2 = -1$.
Powers cycle every 4: $i^1=i,\; i^2=-1,\; i^3=-i,\; i^4=1,\; i^5=i,\;\ldots$
To find $i^n$: divide $n$ by 4 and use the remainder.   $i^{0}=1$ by convention.
Standard form & algebra A complex number has the form $a + bi$ where $a$ (real part) and $b$ (imaginary part) are real.
Add/Subtract: combine real parts and imaginary parts separately.
Multiply: FOIL, then replace $i^2$ with $-1$.
Conjugate of $a+bi$ is $\overline{a+bi} = a-bi$.   Product: $(a+bi)(a-bi) = a^2+b^2$ (real!).
Divide: multiply numerator and denominator by the conjugate of the denominator.
Practice Problems

Enter complex answers as a+bi (e.g. 3+2i, -1-4i, 5i, -i). Powers of i: enter i, -1, -i, or 1. Press Check or hit Enter.
