Quadratics and Parametric Equations

Pre-Calculus  ·  §3.3  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  INSTRUCTOR

A ball is thrown at an angle — its $x$ and $y$ positions each depend on time separately. How can we write two equations in $t$ and then eliminate $t$ to get the path?
Quadratic Applications

Projectile height: $h(t)=-16t^2+v_0 t+h_0$ (in feet)
or $h(t)=-4.9t^2+v_0 t+h_0$ (in meters).

Maximum height: at vertex $t=-\dfrac{v_0}{2(-16)}=\dfrac{v_0}{32}$.

Time to hit ground: set $h(t)=0$, solve via quadratic formula.

Setup: identify $a$, $b$, $c$ from the problem context. The coefficient $a$ is always negative for projectile motion (gravity acts downward).

Parametric Equations

Parametric equations express $x$ and $y$ both as functions of a third variable $t$ (the parameter):

$x=f(t)$, $y=g(t)$

To eliminate the parameter: solve one equation for $t$, substitute into the other.

Example: $x=2t$, $y=t^2$ $\Rightarrow$ $t=\tfrac{x}{2}$ $\Rightarrow$ $y=\tfrac{x^2}{4}$ (parabola).

Worked Examples
Example 1 — Ball height application
A ball is thrown upward from 5 ft at 64 ft/s. Find max height and when it lands.
$h(t)=-16t^2+64t+5$. Max at $t=2$: $h=69$ ft. Lands when $h=0$: $t=\dfrac{-64\pm\sqrt{4096+320}}{-32}\approx 4.08$ s.
Example 2 — Revenue optimization
Revenue is $R(x)=-x^2+120x$ where $x$ is units sold. Find max revenue.
Vertex at $x=60$; $R(60)=-3600+7200=\$3600$.
Example 3 — Eliminate parameter
Eliminate $t$ from $x=t+1$, $y=t^2-2$.
$t=x-1$; $y=(x-1)^2-2=x^2-2x-1$.
Example 4 — Projectile path
A ball: $x=30t$, $y=-16t^2+20t+4$. Eliminate $t$ to get the path.
$t=\dfrac{x}{30}$; $y=-16\left(\dfrac{x}{30}\right)^2+\dfrac{20x}{30}+4=-\dfrac{x^2}{56.25}+\dfrac{2x}{3}+4$.
Practice Problems
1.
A ball is thrown from 3 ft at 48 ft/s. Write $h(t)$ and find max height.
$h(t)=-16t^2+48t+3$; max at $t=1.5$: $h=39$ ft.
2.
Revenue: $R(p)=-5p^2+300p$. Find price $p$ for max revenue.
$p=30$; max $R=\$4500$.
3.
Eliminate $t$: $x=3t-1$, $y=2t+4$.
$t=\dfrac{x+1}{3}$; $y=\dfrac{2(x+1)}{3}+4=\dfrac{2x+14}{3}$.
4.
For $x=\cos t$, $y=\sin t$, what curve is traced?
$x^2+y^2=1$, a unit circle.
5.
A profit model: $P(x)=-2x^2+80x-400$. Find break-even points.
$-2x^2+80x-400=0\Rightarrow x^2-40x+200=0\Rightarrow x=\dfrac{40\pm\sqrt{800}}{2}=20\pm 10\sqrt{2}\approx 5.9,34.1$.
6.
State the vertex of $h(t)=-4.9t^2+14t+1$ and interpret it.
Vertex at $t=\dfrac{14}{9.8}\approx1.43$ s; $h\approx 11$ m — maximum height of the projectile.
