Solving Trig Equations

PreCalc  ·  §7.5  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Two solutions in $[0,2\pi)$ $\cos\theta=k$:   $\theta=\arccos k$   and   $\theta=2\pi-\arccos k$
$\sin\theta=k>0$:   $\theta=\arcsin k$   and   $\theta=\pi-\arcsin k$
$\sin\theta=k<0$:   $\theta=\pi-\arcsin k$   and   $\theta=2\pi+\arcsin k$
$\tan\theta=k$:   one solution $\theta_0$, then $\theta_0+\pi$ (adjust to $[0,2\pi)$)
Unit circle symmetry Cosine solutions are symmetric about the $x$-axis (QI & QIV, or QII & QIII).
Sine solutions are symmetric about the $y$-axis (QI & QII, or QIII & QIV).
Tangent has period $\pi$, so solutions are $\pi$ apart.
Practice Problems

Write π as pi. Two solutions: list in increasing order, comma-separated (e.g. pi/3,5pi/3). One solution: just that value (e.g. pi/2 or 0). Press Check or hit Enter.
