Rational Functions

PreCalc  ·  §3.6  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Domain & vertical asymptotes A rational function is $\dfrac{p(x)}{q(x)}$ where $p,q$ are polynomials, $q\not\equiv 0$.
Domain: all real $x$ except where $q(x) = 0$.
Vertical asymptote at $x = a$ if $q(a) = 0$ and the factor $(x-a)$ does not cancel with the numerator.
Hole (removable discontinuity) at $x = a$ if $(x-a)$ cancels from both numerator and denominator.
Horizontal asymptotes Compare degrees of numerator ($n$) and denominator ($d$):
• $n < d$: horizontal asymptote $y = 0$.
• $n = d$: horizontal asymptote $y = \dfrac{\text{leading coeff of numerator}}{\text{leading coeff of denominator}}$.
• $n > d$: no horizontal asymptote (oblique or none).
