Function Combinations and Composition

PreCalc  ·  §1.4  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Arithmetic combinations $(f+g)(x)=f(x)+g(x)$   $(f-g)(x)=f(x)-g(x)$   $(fg)(x)=f(x)\cdot g(x)$
Evaluate each function separately at the given input, then combine.
Composition $(f \circ g)(x) = f(g(x))$ — apply $g$ first, then feed the result into $f$.
Order matters: $(f\circ g)(a) \neq (g\circ f)(a)$ in general.
$(f\circ g)(3)$: compute $g(3)$ first, then compute $f(\text{that result})$.
