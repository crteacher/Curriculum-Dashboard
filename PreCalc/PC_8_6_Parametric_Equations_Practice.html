Parametric Equations

PreCalc  ·  §8.6  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Parametric equations A curve defined by $x=f(t)$, $y=g(t)$ where $t$ is the parameter.
To eliminate the parameter: solve one equation for $t$, substitute into the other.
Circle: $x=r\cos t$, $y=r\sin t$   $\Rightarrow$   $x^2+y^2=r^2$, radius $r$.
Key parametric curves Ellipse: $x=a\cos t$, $y=b\sin t$   $\Rightarrow$   $\dfrac{x^2}{a^2}+\dfrac{y^2}{b^2}=1$
Shifted circle: $x=h+r\cos t$, $y=k+r\sin t$  —  center $(h,k)$, radius $r$.
Line: $x=x_0+at$, $y=y_0+bt$   $\Rightarrow$   slope $=b/a$.
