Lines

PreCalc  ·  §2.1  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Slope The slope of a line through $(x_1,y_1)$ and $(x_2,y_2)$ is $m = \dfrac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}$  (rise over run).
Positive slope: rises left to right.   Negative slope: falls left to right.
Horizontal line: $m=0$.   Vertical line: $m$ undefined.
Slope-intercept & point-slope forms Slope-intercept: $y = mx + b$ — slope $m$, y-intercept $b$.
Point-slope: $y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$ — slope $m$, passes through $(x_1, y_1)$.
To find $b$: substitute the known point into $y=mx+b$ and solve.
Parallel & perpendicular slopes Parallel lines have equal slopes: $m_1 = m_2$.
Perpendicular lines have slopes that are negative reciprocals: $m_1 \cdot m_2 = -1$, so $m_2 = -\dfrac{1}{m_1}$.
