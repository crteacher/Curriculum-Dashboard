Polynomial Graphs

PreCalc  ·  §3.5  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Behavior at zeros At a zero $x = r$ with multiplicity $m$:
• Odd $m$: graph crosses the x-axis (changes sign).
• Even $m$: graph touches the x-axis and bounces back (same sign on both sides).
Higher multiplicity $\Rightarrow$ flatter touch/crossing.
Graphing from factored form For $f(x) = a(x-r_1)^{m_1}(x-r_2)^{m_2}\cdots$:
1. End behavior from leading term $ax^{\text{degree}}$.
2. y-intercept: $f(0)$.
3. x-intercepts: each $r_i$; cross if $m_i$ odd, touch if $m_i$ even.
4. Sign of $f$ between zeros tells you where the graph is above/below the x-axis.
