Angles and Their Measure

Pre-Calculus  ·  §5.1  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  STUDENT

A clock's minute hand sweeps a full circle in 60 minutes. If you measure the angle in "radians" instead of degrees, what does one full rotation equal?
Degrees and Radians

Degree: one full rotation = $360°$.

Radian: angle subtended by arc length equal to the radius.
One full rotation $= 2\pi$ rad.

Conversion:
Degrees $\to$ radians: multiply by $\dfrac{\pi}{180}$
Radians $\to$ degrees: multiply by $\dfrac{180}{\pi}$

$180°=\pi\text{ rad}$; $90°=\dfrac{\pi}{2}$; $60°=\dfrac{\pi}{3}$; $45°=\dfrac{\pi}{4}$; $30°=\dfrac{\pi}{6}$.

Arc Length, Coterminal Angles

Arc length: $s=r\theta$ ($\theta$ in radians).

Angular speed: $\omega=\dfrac{\theta}{t}$; Linear speed: $v=r\omega$.

Coterminal angles: same initial and terminal sides. Add or subtract $360°$ (or $2\pi$ rad) to find them.

E.g. $410°$ and $50°$ are coterminal; $-\dfrac{\pi}{4}$ and $\dfrac{7\pi}{4}$ are coterminal.

Worked Examples
Example 1 — Convert degrees to radians
Convert $150°$, $270°$, and $-45°$ to radians.
$\dfrac{5\pi}{6}$; $\dfrac{3\pi}{2}$; $-\dfrac{\pi}{4}$.
Example 2 — Convert radians to degrees
Convert $\dfrac{5\pi}{3}$, $\dfrac{7\pi}{4}$, and $3$ rad to degrees.
$300°$; $315°$; $\dfrac{540}{\pi}\approx171.9°$.
Example 3 — Arc length
A circle has radius 6 cm. Find the arc length subtended by an angle of $\dfrac{2\pi}{3}$ rad.
$s=6\cdot\dfrac{2\pi}{3}=4\pi\approx12.6$ cm.
Example 4 — Coterminal angles
Find two angles coterminal with $\dfrac{5\pi}{6}$.
$\dfrac{5\pi}{6}+2\pi=\dfrac{17\pi}{6}$ and $\dfrac{5\pi}{6}-2\pi=-\dfrac{7\pi}{6}$.
Practice Problems
1.
Convert $225°$ to radians.
$\dfrac{5\pi}{4}$.
2.
Convert $\dfrac{11\pi}{6}$ to degrees.
$330°$.
3.
Find arc length: $r=10$, $\theta=\dfrac{\pi}{5}$.
$s=2\pi$.
4.
Find an angle coterminal with $-\dfrac{\pi}{3}$ between $0$ and $2\pi$.
$\dfrac{5\pi}{3}$.
5.
A wheel spins at $2$ rad/s with $r=5$ m. Find linear speed.
$v=10$ m/s.
