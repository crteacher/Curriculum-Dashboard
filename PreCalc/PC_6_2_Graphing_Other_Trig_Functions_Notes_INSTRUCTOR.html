Graphing Other Trig Functions

Pre-Calculus  ·  §6.2  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  INSTRUCTOR

The tangent function has vertical asymptotes — where do they come from, and how does the graph of $\tan x$ compare to those of $\sin x$ and $\cos x$?
Tangent and Cotangent Graphs

$y=\tan x$: period $\pi$; VAs at $x=\frac{\pi}{2}+n\pi$; passes through $(0,0)$; range $(-\infty,\infty)$.

$y=\cot x$: period $\pi$; VAs at $x=n\pi$; passes through $(\pi/2,0)$; range $(-\infty,\infty)$.

For $y=A\tan(B(x-C))+D$:
Period $=\dfrac{\pi}{|B|}$; phase shift $=C$.

Secant and Cosecant Graphs

$y=\sec x=\dfrac{1}{\cos x}$: draw $y=\cos x$, then draw U-shapes touching at each max/min; VAs where $\cos x=0$.

$y=\csc x=\dfrac{1}{\sin x}$: same process with $\sin x$; VAs where $\sin x=0$.

Both have range $(-\infty,-1]\cup[1,\infty)$ and period $2\pi$.

Worked Examples
Example 1 — Tangent graph
Sketch $y=\tan(2x)$ over one period. State period and VAs.
Period $=\pi/2$. VAs at $x=\pm\pi/4,\pm3\pi/4,\ldots$ Passes through $(0,0)$.
Example 2 — Shifted tangent
State the period and VAs of $y=2\tan(x-\dfrac{\pi}{4})$.
Period $=\pi$; shift $\pi/4$ right; VAs at $x=\pi/4+\pi/2+n\pi=\dfrac{3\pi}{4}+n\pi$.
Example 3 — Secant from cosine
Graph $y=\sec x$ by starting from $y=\cos x$.
Draw $y=\cos x$; draw VAs at $x=\pi/2+n\pi$; draw U-curves opening up at each cosine min and down at each cosine max, touching the cosine at its extremes.
Example 4 — Cosecant from sine
Find range and period of $y=2\csc(x)+1$.
Range: $(-\infty,-1]\cup[3,\infty)$ (shifted up 1; amplitude factor 2). Period: $2\pi$.
Practice Problems
1.
Find period of $y=\tan(3x)$.
$\pi/3$.
2.
Where are the VAs of $y=\cot x$?
At $x=0,\pm\pi,\pm2\pi,\ldots$ (i.e. $x=n\pi$).
3.
State range of $y=\sec x$.
$(-\infty,-1]\cup[1,\infty)$.
4.
Find period of $y=\csc(0.5x)$.
$\dfrac{2\pi}{0.5}=4\pi$.
5.
Where are the VAs of $y=\tan(x+\pi/3)$?
$x+\pi/3=\pi/2+n\pi\Rightarrow x=\pi/6+n\pi$.
6.
Sketch key shape: does $y=\tan x$ increase or decrease on each period?
Increases (from $-\infty$ to $+\infty$) on each period between VAs.
