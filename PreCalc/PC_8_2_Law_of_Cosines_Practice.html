Law of Cosines

PreCalc  ·  §8.2  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Law of Cosines $a^2=b^2+c^2-2bc\cos A$   (and cyclic forms for $b^2$, $c^2$)
Rearranged to find an angle: $\cos A=\dfrac{b^2+c^2-a^2}{2bc}$
Use when: given SAS (two sides + included angle) or SSS (all three sides).
Key cosine values $\cos90^{\circ}=0$   $\cos60^{\circ}=\tfrac{1}{2}$   $\cos120^{\circ}=-\tfrac{1}{2}$
$\arccos(0)=90^{\circ}$   $\arccos(\tfrac{1}{2})=60^{\circ}$   $\arccos(-\tfrac{1}{2})=120^{\circ}$
Practice Problems
