Triangles with Inverse Trig

Pre-Calculus  ·  §6.5  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  INSTRUCTOR

Inverse trig gives you an angle from a ratio. But some applied problems give you three parts of a non-right triangle — how do you find the rest?
Right Triangle Review with Inverse Trig

Use right-triangle relationships and inverse trig to find missing angles:

$\theta=\arctan\left(\dfrac{opp}{adj}\right)$, $\theta=\arcsin\left(\dfrac{opp}{hyp}\right)$, etc.

Application setup:

  1. Draw a diagram. Label what you know.
  2. Identify which trig ratio connects the known and unknown values.
  3. Solve; use inverse trig to find angle if needed.
Applications

Height/distance problems: use angle of elevation/depression with $\tan$.

Navigation: bearing problems; set up perpendicular components.

Two-angle problems: two observers see the same object; set up two equations and solve simultaneously.

Worked Examples
Example 1 — Find angle from sides
A right triangle has legs 8 and 15. Find both non-right angles.
Hyp $=17$. $\theta_1=\arctan(8/15)\approx28.07°$; $\theta_2=90°-28.07°=61.93°$.
Example 2 — Height with two angles
Two observers 100 m apart see the top of a pole. Angles of elevation are $35°$ and $52°$. Find the pole height.
Let $d$ = distance from second observer to base: $h=d\tan52°$; $h=(100+d)\tan35°$. Solve: $h\approx139.2$ m (numerical solution).
Example 3 — Composition application
A ramp has length 20 ft and rise 6 ft. Find the angle of inclination.
$\sin\theta=6/20=0.3\Rightarrow\theta=\arcsin(0.3)\approx17.46°$.
Example 4 — Find angle from trig ratio
Simplify and evaluate $\arctan\left(\dfrac{\text{opp}}{\text{adj}}\right)$ for opp $=7$, adj $=24$.
$\arctan(7/24)\approx\arctan(0.292)\approx16.26°$.
Practice Problems
1.
Find angle: right triangle with legs 6 and 8.
$\arctan(6/8)=\arctan(0.75)\approx36.87°$; other angle $\approx53.13°$.
2.
Evaluate $\arccos(0.5)$.
$60°=\pi/3$.
3.
A wire runs from a point on the ground 30 ft from a pole to the top (40 ft high). Find the angle it makes with the ground.
$\arctan(40/30)=\arctan(4/3)\approx53.1°$.
4.
Simplify $\tan(\arcsin(1/2))$.
$\theta=\pi/6$; $\tan(\pi/6)=1/\sqrt3=\sqrt3/3$.
5.
Two buildings are 80 m apart. From the top of the shorter (30 m), angle of elevation to top of taller is $40°$. Find height of taller building.
$h=80\tan40°+30\approx97.2$ m.
6.
Find $\arctan(-\sqrt3)$.
$-\pi/3$ (or $-60°$).
