Unit Circle: Sine and Cosine

PreCalc  ·  §5.2  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Sine and cosine on the unit circle For angle $\theta$ from the positive $x$-axis, the terminal point is $(\cos\theta,\,\sin\theta)$.
Key values: $\sin\dfrac{\pi}{6}=\dfrac{1}{2}$;  $\cos\dfrac{\pi}{3}=\dfrac{1}{2}$;  $\sin\dfrac{\pi}{4}=\cos\dfrac{\pi}{4}=\dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$;  $\sin\dfrac{\pi}{3}=\cos\dfrac{\pi}{6}=\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$
Axes: $\sin 0=0$, $\cos 0=1$;  $\sin\dfrac{\pi}{2}=1$, $\cos\dfrac{\pi}{2}=0$;  $\sin\pi=0$, $\cos\pi=-1$;  $\sin\dfrac{3\pi}{2}=-1$, $\cos\dfrac{3\pi}{2}=0$
Properties ASTC — which functions are positive by quadrant: QI All; QII Sin; QIII Tan; QIV Cos.
Pythagorean identity: $\sin^2\theta+\cos^2\theta=1$
Even/Odd: $\cos(-\theta)=\cos\theta$ (even);  $\sin(-\theta)=-\sin\theta$ (odd)
Period $2\pi$: $\sin(\theta+2\pi)=\sin\theta$; $\cos(\theta+2\pi)=\cos\theta$
