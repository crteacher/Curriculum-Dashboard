Probability with Permutations & Combinations

College Algebra  ·  §S.11  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  STUDENT

You flip a coin 10 times. What is the probability of getting exactly 4 heads? You know the total number of outcomes is $2^{10} = 1024$. You just need the favorable outcomes — and those are exactly the sequences with 4 heads out of 10 flips. Which counting tool gives that number?
The Framework
$$P(\text{event}) = \frac{\text{favorable outcomes}}{\text{total outcomes}}$$

Total outcomes: multiplication principle, $n!$, or $2^n$ for coin flips

Favorable outcomes: $C(n,r)$ or $P(n,r)$ — whether order matters

Coin flip probabilities:

$$P(\text{exactly }k\text{ heads in }n\text{ flips}) = \frac{C(n,k)}{2^n}$$
$$\text{e.g. } P(\text{exactly 4 heads in 10 flips}) = \frac{210}{1024} = \frac{105}{512}$$

At least 1 head:

$$1 - P(\text{0 heads}) = 1 - \frac{1}{2^n}$$
Committee and Selection Problems

One specific person included:

$$P = \frac{C(n-1,\,r-1)}{C(n,r)} = \frac{r}{n} \qquad \text{e.g., 3-from-10: } P = \frac{3}{10}$$

Two specific people both included:

$$P = \frac{C(n-2,\,r-2)}{C(n,r)} = \frac{r(r-1)}{n(n-1)} \qquad \text{e.g., } \frac{C(8,1)}{C(10,3)} = \frac{8}{120} = \frac{1}{15}$$
Ordered Selection (PIN / Password)

Use $P(n,r)$ (permutations) for favorable outcomes when order matters and repetition is not allowed.

$$\text{2-digit PIN, no repeats: } P = \frac{P(10,2)}{10^2} = \frac{90}{100} = \frac{9}{10}$$
$$\text{3-digit PIN, no repeats: } P = \frac{P(10,3)}{10^3} = \frac{720}{1000} = \frac{9}{10}\cdot\frac{8}{10} = \frac{18}{25}$$
Worked Examples
Example 1  —  Exact heads probability
Flip a coin 5 times. Find $P(\text{exactly 2 heads})$.
Favorable: $C(5,2) = 10$; Total: $2^5 = 32$
$P = \frac{10}{32} = \frac{5}{16}$
Example 2  —  Committee membership
A 3-person committee is chosen from 12 people. What is the probability you are on the committee?
$P = \frac{r}{n} = \frac{3}{12} = \frac{1}{4}$
Example 3  —  At least one head — complement
Flip a coin 4 times. Find $P(\text{at least 1 head})$.
$P(\text{all tails}) = \frac{1}{16}$
$P(\text{at least 1 head}) = 1 - \frac{1}{16} = \frac{15}{16}$
Example 4  —  No repeated digits
A 2-digit PIN is chosen at random (digits 0–9, repetition allowed). What is the probability no digit is repeated?
Favorable: $10 \times 9 = 90$; Total: $10^2 = 100$
$P = \frac{90}{100} = \frac{9}{10}$
Practice Problems
1.
Flip a coin 4 times. Find $P(\text{exactly 1 head})$.
$C(4,1)/2^4 = 4/16 = 1/4$
2.
A 2-person committee from 8. $P(\text{you are on it})$?
$r/n = 2/8 = 1/4$
3.
Flip a coin 3 times. Find $P(\text{all tails})$.
$1/2^3 = 1/8$
4.
Flip a coin 3 times. Find $P(\text{at least 1 head})$.
$1 - 1/8 = 7/8$
5.
A 2-person team from 6. $P(\text{you and your friend are both chosen})$?
$C(4,0)/C(6,2) = 1/15$
6.
Flip a coin 4 times. Find $P(\text{exactly 2 heads})$.
$C(4,2)/2^4 = 6/16 = 3/8$
