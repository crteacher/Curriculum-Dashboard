Simplifying Roots

College Algebra  ·  §6.2  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  INSTRUCTOR

Before simplifying $\sqrt{72y^2 x^3}$, what should you look for inside the radical? What makes a factor “come out” of a square root?
Roots as Fractional Exponents

Key equivalence:
$\sqrt[n]{a} = a^{1/n}$

$\sqrt{a} = a^{1/2}$   (square root)
$\sqrt[3]{a} = a^{1/3}$   (cube root)

General rule: $\sqrt[n]{a^m} = a^{m/n}$

Product rule for radicals:
$\sqrt{ab} = \sqrt{a} \cdot \sqrt{b}$
Use this to separate perfect squares.

▸ Show Proof

This is what answers the opening question: it's the exact reason a factor is ever allowed to "come out" of a radical.

By the key equivalence above, $\sqrt{ab}$ is the same thing as $(ab)^{1/2}$.

The "power of a product" rule from the previous section says $(xy)^n=x^ny^n$ for any exponent $n$ — including fractional ones like $\tfrac12$. Applying it here: $(ab)^{1/2}=a^{1/2}\cdot b^{1/2}$.

Converting back to root notation, $a^{1/2}=\sqrt{a}$ and $b^{1/2}=\sqrt{b}$, so $(ab)^{1/2}=\sqrt{a}\cdot\sqrt{b}$. Combining these steps: $\sqrt{ab}=\sqrt{a}\cdot\sqrt{b}$.

This is exactly why a perfect-square factor "comes out": if $a$ happens to be a perfect square, say $a=c^2$, then $\sqrt{c^2\cdot b}=\sqrt{c^2}\cdot\sqrt{b}=c\sqrt{b}$ — the $c^2$ splits off and its square root, $c$, walks out from under the radical.

∎

A factor comes out of a square root when its exponent is at least 2 (or when it contains a perfect square as a factor).

Simplifying — Factor Out Perfect Powers

Steps:

  1. Factor the radicand into (perfect square) × (leftover)
  2. Apply $\sqrt{a^2 \cdot b} = a\sqrt{b}$
  3. Repeat for variables

e.g. $\sqrt{12x^2} = \sqrt{4x^2 \cdot 3} = 2x\sqrt{3}$

e.g. $\sqrt{8x^3} = \sqrt{4x^2 \cdot 2x} = 2x\sqrt{2x}$

e.g. $\sqrt[3]{27x^6} = \sqrt[3]{27} \cdot x^{6/3} = 3x^2$

Worked Examples
Example 1  —  Perfect square
Simplify $\sqrt{25x^2}$.
$\sqrt{25} \cdot \sqrt{x^2} = 5x$
Example 2  —  Factor out perfect square
Simplify $\sqrt{12x^2}$.
$\sqrt{4 \cdot 3 \cdot x^2} = 2x\sqrt{3}$
Example 3  —  Variable with odd exponent
Simplify $\sqrt{8x^3}$.
$\sqrt{4x^2 \cdot 2x} = 2x\sqrt{2x}$
Example 4  —  Cube root
Simplify $\sqrt[3]{27x^6}$.
$\sqrt[3]{27} \cdot \sqrt[3]{x^6} = 3x^2$
