Exponential Functions

College Algebra  ·  §6.3  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  STUDENT

A bacteria colony doubles every hour. What single multiplication is happening at each step? How is this fundamentally different from adding a fixed amount each hour, as in a linear model?
The Exponential Model $y = a \cdot b^x$

$a$ = initial value (when $x = 0$)

$b$ = base = growth/decay factor per step

Each step, multiply by $b$:

  • $x=0$: $y = a$
  • $x=1$: $y = ab$
  • $x=2$: $y = ab^2$
  • $x=n$: $y = ab^n$

e.g. $y = 7 \cdot 3^x$: starts at 7, multiplies by 3 each step

Growth vs. Decay

Growth ($b > 1$): value increases each step

Decay ($0 < b < 1$): value decreases each step

e.g. $b = 2$: doubles each step (growth)
$b = \frac{1}{2}$: halves each step (decay)

Key formula: $y = a \cdot b^x$
where $x$ = number of steps elapsed

e.g. starts at 64 g, loses half per day, after 4 days:
$y = 64 \cdot \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^4 = 64 \cdot \frac{1}{16} = 4$ g

Worked Examples
Example 1  —  Identify a and b
For $y = 7 \cdot 3^x$, identify $a$ and $b$ and describe the behavior.
$a = 7$ (initial value), $b = 3$ (multiplied by 3 each step); growth
Example 2  —  Apply the model
A quantity starts at 4 and triples each step. What is the value after 3 steps?
$y = 4 \cdot 3^3 = 4 \cdot 27 = 108$
Example 3  —  Growth over time
A population starts at 5 and doubles each year. What is it after 10 years?
$y = 5 \cdot 2^{10} = 5 \cdot 1024 = 5120$
Example 4  —  Decay
A substance starts at 64 g and loses half its mass each day. How much remains after 4 days?
$y = 64 \cdot \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^4 = 64 \cdot \frac{1}{16} = 4$ g
Practice Problems
1.
For $y = 3 \cdot 2^x$, what is $a$ and $b$?
$a=3$, $b=2$
2.
A value starts at 10 and doubles each year. What is it after 3 years?
$10 \cdot 2^3 = 80$
3.
A value starts at 100 and is multiplied by $\frac{1}{2}$ each step. What is it after 2 steps?
$100 \cdot \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^2 = 25$
4.
For $y = 2 \cdot 5^x$, what is the value when $x = 0$?
$y = 2 \cdot 1 = 2$
5.
A colony of 3 bacteria triples every hour. How many after 4 hours?
$3 \cdot 3^4 = 3^5 = 243$
6.
Is $y = 6 \cdot (0.8)^x$ growth or decay?
Decay ($b = 0.8 < 1$)
