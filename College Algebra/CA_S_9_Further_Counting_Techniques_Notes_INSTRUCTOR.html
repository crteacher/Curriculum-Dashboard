Further Counting Techniques

College Algebra  ·  §S.9  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  INSTRUCTOR

If you flip a coin 10 times, how many outcomes have exactly 4 heads? Listing them all would take forever — there are $2^{10} = 1024$ total sequences. But notice: you are just choosing which 4 of the 10 slots are heads. The order you pick those slots doesn't matter. What familiar formula does that sound like?
Counting with Combinations

Exactly $k$ heads in $n$ flips: $C(n,k)$
e.g. Exactly 4 heads in 10 flips: $C(10,4) = 210$

At least $k$ heads — direct sum:
$C(n,k) + C(n,k+1) + \cdots + C(n,n)$
e.g. At least 3 heads in 4 flips:
$C(4,3) + C(4,4) = 4 + 1 = 5$

Complement shortcut:
“At least $k$” $= 2^n - $ “fewer than $k$”
e.g. At least 9 heads in 10 flips:
$2^{10} - C(10,0) - C(10,1) = 1024 - 1 - 10 = 1013$

Lattice Paths and Combined Techniques

Lattice paths:
Walking $r$ steps right and $d$ steps down (no backtracking):
Choose which $r$ of the $r+d$ steps go right: $C(r+d, r)$
e.g. 4 right, 3 down: $C(7,4) = C(7,3) = 35$

Combining techniques:
When a problem has two separate groups, count each independently and multiply.
e.g. Exactly 3 heads in 6 flips: $C(6,3) = 20$ sequences

Worked Examples
Example 1  —  Exactly k heads
Flip a coin 6 times. How many outcomes have exactly 2 heads?
$C(6,2) = 15$
Example 2  —  At least k heads — direct sum
Flip a coin 5 times. How many outcomes have at least 4 heads?
$C(5,4) + C(5,5) = 5 + 1 = 6$
Example 3  —  At least k heads — complement
Flip a coin 5 times. How many outcomes have at least 2 heads? (Use the complement.)
Total $= 2^5 = 32$
Fewer than 2: $C(5,0) + C(5,1) = 1 + 5 = 6$
At least 2: $32 - 6 = 26$
Example 4  —  Lattice paths
How many ways can you walk from the top-left to bottom-right of a $3 \times 4$ grid, moving only right or down?
3 right $+$ 4 down $= 7$ total steps
Choose 3 for right: $C(7,3) = 35$
Practice Problems
1.
Flip a coin 5 times. How many outcomes have exactly 3 heads?
$C(5,3) = 10$
2.
Flip a coin 4 times. How many outcomes have at least 3 heads?
$C(4,3)+C(4,4)=4+1=5$
3.
Flip a coin 4 times. How many total outcomes?
$2^4 = 16$
4.
Using the complement: flip 4 coins. How many outcomes have at least 1 head?
$16 - C(4,0) = 16 - 1 = 15$
5.
How many lattice paths go 2 right and 3 down?
$C(5,2) = 10$
6.
Flip 6 coins. How many outcomes have exactly 0 heads?
$C(6,0) = 1$ (all tails)
