Venn Diagrams

Imagine $A$ = cat lovers and $B$ = dog lovers in a class. A Venn diagram has four regions: only $A$, only $B$, both, and neither. What would each region represent in this scenario?
Two-set Venn diagram: four labeled regions
Reading a Venn Diagram

Four regions in a two-set Venn diagram:

  • Only $A$: in $A$ but not $B$
  • $A \cap B$: in both $A$ and $B$
  • Only $B$: in $B$ but not $A$
  • Neither: in $U$ but outside $A \cup B$

Cardinality formulas:
$|A| = $ (only $A$) $+ |A \cap B|$
$|A \cup B| = |A| + |B| - |A \cap B|$
$|U| = |A \cup B| + $ neither

▸ Show Proof

Why $|A \cup B| = |A| + |B| - |A \cap B|$:

Every element of $A \cup B$ falls into exactly one of three regions:

Only $A$: $|A| - |A \cap B|$ elements    Only $B$: $|B| - |A \cap B|$ elements    Both ($A \cap B$): $|A \cap B|$ elements

Adding all three: $(|A|-|A\cap B|)+(|A\cap B|)+(|B|-|A\cap B|) = |A|+|B|-|A\cap B|.$

In other words: adding $|A|$ and $|B|$ counts $|A \cap B|$ twice. Subtracting it once fixes the double-count.

∎

De Morgan's Laws

$(A \cup B)' = A' \cap B'$
Not in $A$ or $B$ → not in $A$ AND not in $B$
(shaded region = neither)

$(A \cap B)' = A' \cup B'$
Not in both → not in $A$ OR not in $B$
(shaded region = everything except the center)

These laws let you distribute complements over union/intersection — and flip the operation.

▸ Show Proof

$(A \cup B)'= A'\cap B'$: Let $x \in U$.

$x \in (A \cup B)'$ $\Leftrightarrow$ $x \notin A \cup B$ $\Leftrightarrow$ $x \notin A$ AND $x \notin B$ $\Leftrightarrow$ $x \in A'$ and $x \in B'$ $\Leftrightarrow$ $x \in A'\cap B'$.

Everyday: "not in A or B" = "not in A, and not in B."

$(A \cap B)'= A'\cup B'$:

$x \in (A \cap B)'$ $\Leftrightarrow$ $x \notin A \cap B$ $\Leftrightarrow$ $x \notin A$ OR $x \notin B$ $\Leftrightarrow$ $x \in A'$ or $x \in B'$ $\Leftrightarrow$ $x \in A'\cup B'$.

Everyday: "not in both A and B" = "missing from A, or missing from B."

∎

Worked Examples
Example 1  —  Read cardinalities from a diagram
Only $A = 5$, $A \cap B = 3$, only $B = 4$, neither $= 2$. Find $|A|$, $|B|$, $|A \cup B|$, and $|U|$.
$|A|=8$   $|B|=7$   $|A \cup B|=12$   $|U|=14$
Example 2  —  Complement cardinalities
Using the same diagram above, find $|A'|$ and $|(A \cup B)'|$.
$|A'| = |U| - |A| = 14 - 8 = 6$
$|(A \cup B)'| = $ neither $= 2$
Example 3  —  Apply De Morgan's Law
State $(A \cup B)'$ using De Morgan's Law.
$(A \cup B)' = A' \cap B'$
Example 4  —  Only-A cardinality
Using the diagram from Example 1, find $|A \setminus B|$ (only $A$).
$|A \setminus B| = |A| - |A \cap B| = 8 - 3 = 5$
Practice Problems

$4 + 6 + 2 = 12$

$12 + 3 = 15$

$7 + 3 = 10$
6.

$10 + 11 - |A \cap B| = 18 \Rightarrow |A \cap B| = 3$
