Completing the Square

College Algebra  ·  §4.3  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  INSTRUCTOR

The identity $x^2 + bx = \left(x + \frac{b}{2}\right)^2 - \left(\frac{b}{2}\right)^2$ turns a binomial into a perfect square minus a constant. What does that tell you about $x^2 + 6x$, and why is that useful for solving equations?
The Completing-the-Square Identity

Key identity:
$x^2 + bx = \left(x + \dfrac{b}{2}\right)^2 - \left(\dfrac{b}{2}\right)^2$

Steps to rewrite $x^2 + bx + c$ in vertex form:

  1. Take half of the $x$-coefficient: $\dfrac{b}{2}$
  2. Square it: $\left(\dfrac{b}{2}\right)^2$
  3. Add and subtract that value
  4. Write as $(x + \frac{b}{2})^2 + \text{remainder}$

e.g. $x^2 + 8x = (x+4)^2 - 16$

Solving with the Identity

Solve $x^2 + bx + c = 0$:

  1. Move constant: $x^2 + bx = -c$
  2. Complete the square on left, add same to right
  3. Write as perfect square: $(x+h)^2 = k$
  4. Take square root: $x + h = \pm\sqrt{k}$
  5. Solve for $x$

e.g. $(x+3)^2 = 16$:
$x + 3 = \pm 4 \Rightarrow x = 1$ or $x = -7$

Worked Examples
Example 1  —  Rewrite as a perfect square
Rewrite $x^2 + 8x$ by completing the square.
$\left(\frac{8}{2}\right)^2 = 16$;   $x^2 + 8x = (x+4)^2 - 16$
Example 2  —  Solve by completing the square
Solve $x^2 + 6x + 5 = 0$ by completing the square.
$x^2+6x=-5 \Rightarrow (x+3)^2=4 \Rightarrow x+3=\pm 2$
$x=-1$ or $x=-5$
Example 3  —  Negative constant
Solve $x^2 - 4x - 5 = 0$ by completing the square.
$x^2-4x=5 \Rightarrow (x-2)^2=9 \Rightarrow x-2=\pm 3$
$x=5$ or $x=-1$
Example 4  —  Already in vertex form
Solve $(x+3)^2 = 16$.
$x+3=\pm 4 \Rightarrow x=1$ or $x=-7$
Practice Problems
1.
Rewrite $x^2 + 10x$ by completing the square.
$(x+5)^2 - 25$
2.
Rewrite $x^2 - 6x$ by completing the square.
$(x-3)^2 - 9$
3.
Solve $x^2 + 4x - 12 = 0$ by completing the square.
$(x+2)^2=16 \Rightarrow x=2$ or $x=-6$
4.
Solve $x^2 - 8x + 7 = 0$ by completing the square.
$(x-4)^2=9 \Rightarrow x=7$ or $x=1$
5.
Solve $(x-5)^2 = 9$.
$x-5=\pm 3 \Rightarrow x=8$ or $x=2$
6.
Solve $x^2 + 2x - 3 = 0$ by completing the square.
$(x+1)^2=4 \Rightarrow x=1$ or $x=-3$
