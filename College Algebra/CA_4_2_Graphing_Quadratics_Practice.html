Graphing Quadratics

College Algebra  ·  §4.2  ·  Instructor: Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Standard Form f(x) = ax² + bx + c Vertex x-coordinate: $h = -b\,/\,(2a)$, then $k = f(h)$. Opens up if $a>0$ (minimum), down if $a<0$ (maximum). y-intercept is always $(0, c)$.
Vertex Form f(x) = a(x − h)² + k Vertex $(h, k)$ read directly. Watch the sign: $x+2 = x-(-2)$, so $h=-2$. y-intercept: substitute $x=0$ to get $ah^2+k$.
Practice Problems

Graph problems: read directly from the grid or enter up/down. Text problems: integers for coordinates; max or min where asked. Press Check or hit Enter.
