Exponential Functions

College Algebra  ·  §6.3  ·  Instructor: Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Exponential Model $y = a \cdot b^x$.   $a$ is the initial value ($y$ when $x=0$).   $b$ is the base — the number you multiply by each step.
Growth vs. Decay $b > 1$: output grows as $x$ increases.   $0 < b < 1$: output decays (shrinks) as $x$ increases.
Evaluating the Model Substitute the known $x$ into $y = a \cdot b^x$. Raising $b$ to a power means multiplying $b$ by itself $x$ times.
