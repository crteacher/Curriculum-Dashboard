Solving Equations with One Unknown

College Algebra  ·  §1.1  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  INSTRUCTOR

Solving an equation means finding the value that makes both sides balance. Every operation you perform must be done to both sides to keep the balance. What property of equality justifies that idea?
The Tools

Distributive Property:
$a(b+c)=ab+ac$
$(a+b)(c+d)=ac+ad+bc+bd$

Inverse Operations:
Undo addition → subtract
Undo multiplication → divide

Goal: isolate the variable by undoing operations in reverse PEMDAS order.

▸ Common Mistake

Distribution hits every term inside the parentheses, and a leading minus sign is a factor of $-1$ that flips the sign of each term. The classic error is writing $-(x-3)=-x-3$; it's actually $-x+3$. The same slip appears as $2(x+5)=2x+5$, forgetting to multiply the $5$. Multiply the outside factor onto each interior term separately, signs included.

Strategy: distribute → combine like terms → move variable terms to one side → move constants to the other → divide.

Number of Solutions

Exactly one solution: variable remains → solve for $x$
e.g. $3x+4=2x-4 \Rightarrow x=-8$

Infinitely many solutions:
variable cancels → TRUE statement ($0=0$)
e.g. $3x+3=3(x+1) \Rightarrow 3=3$ ✓

No solution:
variable cancels → FALSE statement ($a=b$, $a\neq b$)
e.g. $5x-7=5x+2 \Rightarrow -7=2$ ✗

▸ Common Mistake

When the variable cancels, the work isn't over and the answer isn't automatically "no solution." Read what's left: a true statement like $3=3$ means infinitely many solutions, while a false one like $-7=2$ means no solution. Students also reach $0=0$ and write "$x=0$" — but $0=0$ says nothing about $x$; it signals that every $x$ works. Match the leftover statement to the right conclusion instead of guessing from the fact that $x$ disappeared.

Worked Examples
Example 1  —  Fraction coefficient
Solve: $0.5(x+3)=7$
$x+3=14 \Rightarrow x=11$
Example 2  —  Variables on both sides
Solve: $3x+4=2x-4$
$x+4=-4 \Rightarrow x=-8$
Example 3  —  Distribution + combine
Solve: $2(x+5)-x=13$
$2x+10-x=13 \Rightarrow x+10=13 \Rightarrow x=3$
Example 4  —  No solution
Solve: $5x-7=5x+2$
$-7=2$ — FALSE → No solution
