Slope-Intercept & Point-Slope Forms

College Algebra  ·  §2.2  ·  Instructor: Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Slope-Intercept Form $y = mx + b$   — use when you know slope $m$ and y-intercept $b$ directly.
Point-Slope Form $y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$   — use when you know slope $m$ and any point $(x_1, y_1)$. Convert to slope-intercept by distributing and solving for $y$.
