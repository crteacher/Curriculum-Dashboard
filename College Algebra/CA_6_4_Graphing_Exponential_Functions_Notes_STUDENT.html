Graphing Exponential Functions

College Algebra  ·  §6.4  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  STUDENT

The function $y = ab^x$ passes through $(0, a)$. How is the shape of this curve fundamentally different from a line or a parabola as $x$ increases?
Key Features of $y = a \cdot b^x$

y-intercept: $(0, a)$ — always

Multiply-by-$b$ pattern: each unit increase in $x$ multiplies $y$ by $b$

Horizontal asymptote: $y = 0$ (curve approaches but never touches)

Domain: $(-\infty, \infty)$

Range: $(0, \infty)$ when $a > 0$

▸ Show Proof

y-intercept is $(0, a)$: Plug $x = 0$ into $y = a \cdot b^x$:

$$y = a \cdot b^0 = a \cdot 1 = a$$

Since $b^0 = 1$ for any valid base ($b \neq 0$), the initial value $a$ is always the $y$-intercept — no matter what the base is.

Horizontal asymptote is $y = 0$: For $a > 0$ and $b > 1$, the function grows without bound as $x \to +\infty$. But as $x \to -\infty$, $b^x = 1/b^{|x|} \to 0$ because we're dividing by larger and larger numbers. The function approaches $0$ but never equals $0$, because $b^x > 0$ for all real $x$ — no finite power of a positive number is zero. Hence $y = 0$ is a horizontal asymptote, never reached.

∎

To build a table: start at $(0, a)$, then multiply by $b$ for each step right.

Growth vs. Decay Shape

$b > 1$ (growth):
Curve rises steeply to the right.
Falls toward $y=0$ as $x \to -\infty$.

$0 < b < 1$ (decay):
Curve falls toward $y=0$ as $x \to +\infty$.
Rises steeply as $x \to -\infty$.

e.g. $y = 3 \cdot 2^x$: table at $x = 0,1,2,3$
$y = 3, 6, 12, 24$

Worked Examples
Example 1  —  y-intercept
State the y-intercept of $y = 5 \cdot 3^x$.
$(0, 5)$
Example 2  —  Extend a pattern
$y = 4 \cdot 2^x$ passes through $(3, 32)$. What is $y$ when $x = 4$?
Multiply by $2$: $y = 32 \cdot 2 = 64$
Example 3  —  Identify decay from graph
A curve falls toward $y = 0$ as $x$ increases. What does that tell you about $b$?
$b < 1$ — the function is exponential decay
Example 4  —  Build a table
Build a table of values for $y = 3 \cdot 2^x$ at $x = 0, 1, 2, 3$.
$x=0: 3$    $x=1: 6$    $x=2: 12$    $x=3: 24$
Practice Problems
1.
State the y-intercept of $y = 4 \cdot 5^x$.
$(0, 4)$
2.
For $y = 2 \cdot 3^x$, what is $y$ when $x = 3$?
$2 \cdot 27 = 54$
3.
For $y = 10 \cdot (0.5)^x$, is this growth or decay?
Decay ($b=0.5<1$)
4.
What is the horizontal asymptote of $y = 6 \cdot 4^x$?
$y = 0$
5.
Build a table for $y = 5 \cdot 2^x$ at $x = 0, 1, 2$.
$x=0: 5$; $x=1: 10$; $x=2: 20$
6.
State the domain and range of $y = 3 \cdot 7^x$.
Domain: $(-\infty,\infty)$; Range: $(0,\infty)$
