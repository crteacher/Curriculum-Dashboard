Multiply & Divide Complex Numbers

College Algebra  ·  §5.2  ·  Instructor: Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Multiplying Complex Numbers Use FOIL, then replace $i^2$ with $-1$. $(a+bi)(c+di) = (ac-bd)+(ad+bc)i$.
Complex Conjugate The conjugate of $a+bi$ is $a-bi$. Their product $(a+bi)(a-bi)=a^2+b^2$ is always a real number.
Dividing Complex Numbers Multiply numerator and denominator by the conjugate of the denominator. This clears $i$ from the denominator: $\dfrac{a+bi}{c+di} = \dfrac{(a+bi)(c-di)}{c^2+d^2}$.
