Complex Numbers

College Algebra  ·  §5.1  ·  Instructor: Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Imaginary Unit $i = \sqrt{-1}$, so $i^2 = -1$. Simplify $\sqrt{-k} = \sqrt{k}\cdot i$. Powers cycle: $i^1=i,\; i^2=-1,\; i^3=-i,\; i^4=1$, then repeat.
Complex Number a + bi Add/subtract by combining real and imaginary parts separately: $(a+bi)+(c+di)=(a+c)+(b+d)i$.
Quadratic Formula with D < 0 When $D = b^2-4ac < 0$, write $\sqrt{D} = \sqrt{|D|}\cdot i$. The two solutions are complex conjugates: $x = \dfrac{-b \pm \sqrt{|D|}\,i}{2a}$.
Practice Problems

Integer answers throughout. For coefficient-of-i questions enter just the number (e.g. 3 or -2). For i² and i⁴ enter -1 or 1. Press Check or hit Enter.
