Permutations and Combinations

College Algebra  ·  §S.8  ·  Instructor: Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Permutations — Order Matters $P(n,r) = \dfrac{n!}{(n-r)!} = n(n-1)\cdots(n-r+1)$.   Use when positions or roles are distinct.
Combinations — Order Doesn't Matter $C(n,r) = \dfrac{n!}{r!\,(n-r)!} = \dfrac{P(n,r)}{r!}$.   Use when you are selecting a group, hand, or team with no assigned roles.
Symmetry Rule $C(n,r) = C(n,\,n-r)$.   Choosing $r$ objects to keep is the same count as choosing $n-r$ to leave out.
