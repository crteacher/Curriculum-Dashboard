Multiplication Principle

College Algebra  ·  §S.7  ·  Instructor: Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Multiplication Principle If task 1 has $n_1$ options and task 2 has $n_2$ options (independently), the total number of outcomes is $n_1 \times n_2$. Extends to any number of consecutive choices.
Factorial $n! = n \times (n-1) \times \cdots \times 2 \times 1$.   $0! = 1$.   The number of ways to arrange $n$ distinct objects in a row is $n!$
Ordered Selection (no repeats) Choosing $r$ positions from $n$ objects where order matters and repeats are not allowed: $n \times (n-1) \times \cdots \times (n-r+1)$.
