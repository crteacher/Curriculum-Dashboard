Simplifying Roots

College Algebra  ·  §6.2  ·  Instructor: Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Roots as Fractional Exponents $\sqrt[n]{a} = a^{1/n}$.   $\sqrt[n]{a^m} = a^{m/n}$. All exponent rules apply — the denominator is the root index, the numerator is the power.
Simplifying Strategy Factor the radicand into (largest perfect power) × (remainder). Pull the perfect power outside: $\sqrt{a^2 b} = a\sqrt{b}$.
Key Facts $\sqrt{x^{2n}} = x^n$.   $\sqrt[3]{x^{3n}} = x^n$.   $\sqrt{a \cdot b} = \sqrt{a}\cdot\sqrt{b}$.
