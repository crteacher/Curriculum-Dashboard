Partitions

College Algebra  ·  §S.10  ·  Instructor: Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Ordered Partition (Labeled Groups) Split $n$ objects into labeled groups of sizes $n_1, n_2, \ldots$: $\dfrac{n!}{n_1!\,n_2!\cdots} = C(n,n_1)\times C(n-n_1,n_2)\times\cdots$
Unordered Partition (Identical Groups) Split $n$ objects into $m$ interchangeable groups of size $r$: divide the ordered count by $m!$ to remove duplicate groupings.
The Key Question Do the groups have distinct identities (labeled destinations, roles, colors)? → Ordered.   Are the groups interchangeable? → Unordered (divide by $m!$).
