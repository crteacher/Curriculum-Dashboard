Compound Inequalities

College Algebra  ·  §1.4  ·  Instructor: Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
AND — Intersection Both conditions must hold simultaneously. Solve each part; the solution is the overlap. Three-part form: operate on all three sections at once.   Example: $2 < 3x - 1 < 8 \;\Rightarrow\; 1 < x < 3 \;\Rightarrow\; (1,3)$
OR — Union At least one condition holds. Solve each part separately; combine with $\cup$.   Example: $x < 2 \text{ OR } x > 5 \;\Rightarrow\; (-\infty,2)\cup(5,\infty)$
The Flip Rule Multiply or divide both sides by a negative number → flip every inequality sign in the expression. In a three-part inequality, both signs flip.   Example: $4 < {-2x} + 8 < 10 \;\Rightarrow\; -4 < {-2x} < 2 \;\Rightarrow\; 2 > x > -1$
