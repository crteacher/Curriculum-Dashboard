Applications

College Algebra  ·  §1.5  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  INSTRUCTOR

A streaming service costs $8 per month plus a $20 sign-up fee. A competitor charges $13 per month with no sign-up fee. After how many months does the first service become cheaper? What equation models this situation?
Problem-Solving Strategy

4-Step Method:

  1. Define the variable (what are you finding?)
  2. Write an equation or inequality from the words
  3. Solve algebraically
  4. Interpret the answer in context

Key phrases:
“at most” → $\leq$   “at least” → $\geq$
“more than” → $>$   “fewer than” → $<$
“total” → addition   “per” → multiplication

Common Setups

Flat fee + rate: $\text{cost} = \text{fee} + r \cdot n$

Break-even: set two cost expressions equal

Budget: $\text{cost} \leq \text{budget}$, solve for quantity

Savings goal: $\text{saved} + \text{rate} \cdot n \geq \text{goal}$

Consecutive integers: $n, n+1, n+2, \ldots$

Perimeter: $P = 2l + 2w$

Worked Examples
Example 1  —  Flat fee + rate
Tickets cost $9 each plus a $6 service fee. Mia paid $42. How many tickets did she buy?
Let $n$ = tickets. $9n + 6 = 42 \Rightarrow 9n = 36 \Rightarrow n = 4$ tickets
Example 2  —  Break-even
Gym A: $10/month + $50 join fee. Gym B: $20/month. After how many months is Gym A cheaper?
$10n + 50 = 20n \Rightarrow 50 = 10n \Rightarrow n = 5$ months
Example 3  —  Budget inequality
You have $47 to spend. Games cost $8 each. At most how many games can you buy?
$8n \leq 47 \Rightarrow n \leq 5.875 \Rightarrow$ at most 5 games
Example 4  —  Savings goal
You have $30 saved and save $25 per week. How many weeks until you have at least $155?
$30 + 25n \geq 155 \Rightarrow 25n \geq 125 \Rightarrow n \geq 5$ weeks
Practice Problems
1.
A plumber charges $50 plus $40 per hour. The bill was $170. How many hours did the job take?
Let $h$ = hours. $50 + 40h = 170 \Rightarrow 40h = 120 \Rightarrow h = 3$ hours
2.
Plan A: $15/month. Plan B: $25/month with a $40 rebate after 6 months. Which is cheaper over 6 months?
Plan A: $90. Plan B: $150 − $40 = $110. Plan A is cheaper.
3.
You can spend at most $60 on books at $7 each. What is the maximum number of books?
$7n \leq 60 \Rightarrow n \leq 8.57 \Rightarrow$ at most 8 books
4.
Three consecutive integers sum to 48. Find them.
$n + (n+1) + (n+2) = 48 \Rightarrow 3n + 3 = 48 \Rightarrow n = 15$; integers: 15, 16, 17
5.
You need to earn more than $200 this week. You already earned $80 and make $12/hour. How many more hours?
$80 + 12h > 200 \Rightarrow 12h > 120 \Rightarrow h > 10$; more than 10 hours
6.
A rectangle has perimeter 36 cm. The length is twice the width. Find the dimensions.
$2(2w) + 2w = 36 \Rightarrow 6w = 36 \Rightarrow w = 6$, $l = 12$ cm
