Slope-Intercept and Point-Slope Forms

College Algebra  ·  §2.2  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  STUDENT

A line passes through $(3, 7)$ with slope 2. How do you write the equation of this line? What information do you need, and which form uses it most directly?
Slope-Intercept Form

$y = mx + b$
$m$ = slope    $b$ = y-intercept

Use when you know slope and y-intercept.
e.g. $m = -3$, $b = 5 \Rightarrow y = -3x + 5$

Converting standard form:
Solve for $y$.
e.g. $6x - 3y = 9$:
$-3y = -6x + 9 \Rightarrow y = 2x - 3$

Slope-intercept form is best for graphing and identifying slope quickly.

Point-Slope Form

$y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$
$(x_1, y_1)$ = known point on the line

Use when you know slope and a point.
e.g. $m = 4$, point $(1, 3)$:
$y - 3 = 4(x - 1) \Rightarrow y = 4x - 1$

Two points given:
Find slope first, then apply point-slope.
e.g. $(2,1)$ and $(4,7)$:
$m = \frac{7-1}{4-2} = 3$, then $y-1=3(x-2) \Rightarrow y=3x-5$

Worked Examples
Example 1  —  Slope-intercept from m and b
Write the equation of a line with $m = -3$ and $b = 5$.
$y = -3x + 5$
Example 2  —  Convert from standard form
Convert $6x - 3y = 9$ to slope-intercept form.
$-3y = -6x + 9 \Rightarrow y = 2x - 3$
Example 3  —  Point-slope form
Write the equation of the line with $m = 4$ through $(1, 3)$.
$y - 3 = 4(x - 1) \Rightarrow y = 4x - 1$
Example 4  —  Line through two points
Write the equation of the line through $(2, 1)$ and $(4, 7)$.
$m = \frac{6}{2} = 3$; $y - 1 = 3(x-2) \Rightarrow y = 3x - 5$
Practice Problems
1.
Write the equation of the line with $m = 2$ and $b = -4$.
$y = 2x - 4$
2.
Convert $4x + 2y = 8$ to slope-intercept form.
$y = -2x + 4$
3.
Write the equation with $m = -1$ through $(0, 6)$.
$y = -x + 6$
4.
Write the equation with $m = 3$ through $(2, 5)$.
$y - 5 = 3(x-2) \Rightarrow y = 3x - 1$
5.
Write the equation of the line through $(1, 4)$ and $(3, 10)$.
$m=3$; $y=3x+1$
6.
Write the equation of the line through $(-1, 2)$ and $(3, -2)$.
$m=-1$; $y=-x+1$
