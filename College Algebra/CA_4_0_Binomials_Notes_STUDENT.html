Binomials & Polynomial Multiplication

College Algebra  ·  §4.0  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  STUDENT

FOIL is a memory device for applying the distributive property twice. When you multiply $(a+b)(c+d)$, how many individual products do you get, and which letter of FOIL produces the constant term?
FOIL — General Binomials

$(a+b)(c+d) = ac + ad + bc + bd$

First  ·  Outer  ·  Inner  ·  Last

e.g. $(x+2)(x+7)$:
F: $x \cdot x = x^2$
O: $x \cdot 7 = 7x$
I: $2 \cdot x = 2x$
L: $2 \cdot 7 = 14$
$= x^2 + 9x + 14$

Combine like terms (the outer and inner products) to get the final trinomial.

Special Patterns

Difference of squares:
$(a+b)(a-b) = a^2 - b^2$
e.g. $(x+6)(x-6) = x^2 - 36$

Perfect square trinomial:
$(a+b)^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2$
$(a-b)^2 = a^2 - 2ab + b^2$
e.g. $(x+5)^2 = x^2 + 10x + 25$

Tip: recognizing these patterns saves time and prevents sign errors.

Worked Examples
Example 1  —  Basic FOIL
Expand $(x+2)(x+7)$.
$x^2 + 7x + 2x + 14 = x^2 + 9x + 14$
Example 2  —  Mixed signs
Expand $(x-3)(x+8)$.
$x^2 + 8x - 3x - 24 = x^2 + 5x - 24$
Example 3  —  Difference of squares
Expand $(x+6)(x-6)$.
$(x+6)(x-6) = x^2 - 36$
Example 4  —  Perfect square
Expand $(x+5)^2$.
$(x+5)^2 = x^2 + 10x + 25$
