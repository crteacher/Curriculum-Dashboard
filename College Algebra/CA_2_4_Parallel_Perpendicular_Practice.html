Parallel and Perpendicular Lines

College Algebra  ·  §2.4  ·  Instructor: Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Parallel Lines Same slope, different y-intercepts: $m_1 = m_2$. They never intersect.
Perpendicular Lines Negative reciprocal slopes: $m_1 \cdot m_2 = -1$. Flip it and change the sign. Exception: horizontal ($y=k$) is perpendicular to vertical ($x=h$).
