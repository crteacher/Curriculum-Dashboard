Probability with Permutations and Combinations

College Algebra  ·  §S.11  ·  Instructor: Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
The Framework $P(\text{event}) = \dfrac{\text{favorable outcomes}}{\text{total outcomes}}$. Use $C(n,r)$ or $P(n,r)$ to count each, depending on whether order matters.
Coin Flip Probabilities $P(\text{exactly }k\text{ heads in }n\text{ flips}) = \dfrac{C(n,k)}{2^n}$.   Use the complement for "at least" questions: $1 - P(\text{fewer than }k)$.
Committee Membership $P(\text{specific person on committee of }r\text{ from }n) = \dfrac{r}{n}$.   $P(\text{specific pair both included}) = \dfrac{r(r-1)}{n(n-1)}$.
