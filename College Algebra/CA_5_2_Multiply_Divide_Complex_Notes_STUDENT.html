Multiply & Divide Complex Numbers

College Algebra  ·  §5.2  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  STUDENT

What would you multiply $(3 + 2i)$ by to get a purely real number? What special relationship does that multiplier have to $3 + 2i$?
Multiplying Complex Numbers

Use FOIL, then replace $i^2 = -1$.

e.g. $(2+i)(3-2i)$:
F: $6$   O: $-4i$   I: $3i$   L: $-2i^2 = 2$
$= 8 - i$

Conjugate pair → real result:
$(a+bi)(a-bi) = a^2 + b^2$
e.g. $(4+3i)(4-3i) = 16+9 = 25$

Squaring:
$(a+bi)^2 = a^2 + 2abi + b^2 i^2 = (a^2-b^2) + 2abi$
e.g. $(2+i)^2 = 3+4i$

Conjugates and Division

Complex conjugate of $a+bi$: $a-bi$

To divide $\dfrac{a+bi}{c+di}$:
Multiply numerator and denominator by the conjugate of the denominator $c-di$.

e.g. $\dfrac{7+i}{2+i}$:
$= \dfrac{(7+i)(2-i)}{(2+i)(2-i)} = \dfrac{14-7i+2i-i^2}{4+1} = \dfrac{15-5i}{5} = 3-i$

Worked Examples
Example 1  —  Multiply with FOIL
Multiply $(2+i)(3-2i)$.
F: $6$, O: $-4i$, I: $3i$, L: $-2i^2=2$
$= 8 - i$
Example 2  —  Conjugate pair
Multiply $(4+3i)(4-3i)$.
$a^2+b^2 = 16+9 = 25$
Example 3  —  Division by complex number
Compute $\dfrac{7+i}{2+i}$.
$\dfrac{(7+i)(2-i)}{5} = \dfrac{15-5i}{5} = 3-i$
Example 4  —  Square a complex number
Compute $(2+i)^2$.
$(2+i)^2 = 4 + 4i + i^2 = 4 + 4i - 1 = 3+4i$
