Basic Integration Rules

Calculus I  ·  §4.3  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  INSTRUCTOR

Riemann sums let us approximate $\int_a^b f(x)\,dx$, but computing thousands of rectangles by hand is impractical. Can we reverse the differentiation rules to evaluate integrals exactly? Every derivative rule has a mirror — what does the Power Rule look like going backwards?
Integration Rules

Power Rule $(n\neq -1)$:

$$\int x^n\,dx=\frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1}+C$$

Constant Rule: $\displaystyle\int k\,dx=kx+C$

Constant Multiple: $\displaystyle\int c\,f(x)\,dx=c\int f(x)\,dx$

Sum / Difference:

$\displaystyle\int[f(x)\pm g(x)]\,dx=\int f\,dx\pm\int g\,dx$

$+C$ is the constant of integration — every antiderivative family has infinitely many members differing by a constant. For definite integrals, $C$ cancels and can be dropped.

Common Antiderivatives
  • $\displaystyle\int e^x\,dx = e^x+C$
  • $\displaystyle\int \frac{1}{x}\,dx = \ln|x|+C$
  • $\displaystyle\int \sin x\,dx = -\cos x+C$
  • $\displaystyle\int \cos x\,dx = \sin x+C$

Area below the $x$-axis is negative.
For a definite integral, any region where $f(x)<0$ subtracts from the total. Signed area $\neq$ total area.

Worked Examples
Example 1  —  Power Rule (indefinite)
Find $\displaystyle\int (3x^2+4x)\,dx$.
$\dfrac{3x^3}{3}+\dfrac{4x^2}{2}+C = x^3+2x^2+C$
Example 2  —  Polynomial antiderivative
Find $\displaystyle\int (5x^4-6x+2)\,dx$.
$x^5-3x^2+2x+C$
Example 3  —  Definite integral
Evaluate $\displaystyle\int_0^3 x^2\,dx$.
$\left[\dfrac{x^3}{3}\right]_0^3=\dfrac{27}{3}-0=9$
Example 4  —  Mixed antiderivatives
Find $\displaystyle\int (4e^x+3\cos x)\,dx$.
$4e^x+3\sin x+C$
Practice Problems
1.
Find $\displaystyle\int x^5\,dx$.
$\dfrac{x^6}{6}+C$
2.
Find $\displaystyle\int (6x^2+4x-3)\,dx$.
$2x^3+2x^2-3x+C$
3.
Evaluate $\displaystyle\int_0^2 4x^3\,dx$.
$\left[x^4\right]_0^2=16$
4.
Find $\displaystyle\int (2e^x-\sin x)\,dx$.
$2e^x+\cos x+C$
5.
Evaluate $\displaystyle\int_1^4 \sqrt{x}\,dx$.
$\left[\dfrac{2x^{3/2}}{3}\right]_1^4=\dfrac{16}{3}-\dfrac{2}{3}=\dfrac{14}{3}$
6.
Is $\displaystyle\int_{-2}^{0} x^3\,dx$ positive or negative? Why?
Negative — $x^3<0$ on $(-2,0)$, so the signed area is below the $x$-axis.
