Rates of Change

Calculus I  ·  §2.4.5  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  STUDENT

We know $f'(x)$ gives the slope of a graph. But what does a derivative mean in the real world? If $s(t)$ is position, what does $s'(t)$ tell us — and why does the sign matter?
Position, Velocity, Acceleration

If $s(t)$ = position at time $t$, then:

$v(t)=s'(t)$   (velocity = rate of change of position)
$a(t)=v'(t)=s''(t)$   (acceleration)

Sign of velocity:

  • $v(t)>0$   object moving forward
  • $v(t)<0$   object moving backward
  • $v(t)=0$   object momentarily at rest

Speed $=|v(t)|$   (always non-negative)

Finding when object is at rest: set $v(t)=0$ and solve for $t$. The sign of $v$ on either side tells you whether the object reverses direction.

Other Rates of Change

The derivative = instantaneous rate of change. This applies to any quantity, not just position.

Marginal cost:
If $C(x)$ = cost to produce $x$ units, then $C'(x)$ = cost of one more unit (approximately, when production is already at $x$).

Population, temperature, revenue — any quantity changing over time has a derivative that measures its rate of change.

Units always matter: if $s$ is in meters and $t$ in seconds, then $v=ds/dt$ is in m/s and $a=dv/dt$ is in m/s². The derivative units = (output units) / (input units).

When $s(t)$ rises, $v(t)=s'(t)>0$ (moving forward); when $s(t)$ falls, $v(t)<0$ (moving backward). At the peak, $v=0$.
Worked Examples
Example 1  —  Find velocity from position
Find $v(t)$ for $s(t)=3t^2+4t$.
$v(t)=s'(t)=6t+4$
Example 2  —  Find acceleration from position
Find $a(t)$ for $s(t)=2t^3-3t^2$.
$v(t)=s'(t)=6t^2-6t$
$a(t)=v'(t)=12t-6$
Example 3  —  When is the object at rest?
Find when $v(t)=0$ for $s(t)=t^2-6t$.
$v(t)=2t-6$  ⇒  $2t-6=0$  ⇒  $t=3$
$v<0$ for $t<3$ (moving backward); $v>0$ for $t>3$ (moving forward).
Example 4  —  Marginal cost
Find $C'(x)$ and $C'(10)$ for $C(x)=2x^2+5x$.
$C'(x)=4x+5$
$C'(10)=4(10)+5=45$
Producing the 11th unit costs approximately $\$45$.
Practice Problems
1.
Find $v(t)$ for $s(t)=5t^2+2t$.
$v(t)=10t+2$
2.
Find $v(2)$ for $s(t)=3t^2+t$.
$v(t)=6t+1$;   $v(2)=13$
3.
Find $a(t)$ for $s(t)=t^3+2t^2$.
$v(t)=3t^2+4t$;   $a(t)=6t+4$
4.
Find $a(t)$ for $v(t)=4t+3$.
$a(t)=4$
5.
Find $t$ when $v=0$ for $s(t)=t^2-8t$.
$v(t)=2t-8=0 \Rightarrow t=4$
6.
Find $C'(5)$ for $C(x)=3x^2+7x$.
$C'(x)=6x+7$;   $C'(5)=37$
