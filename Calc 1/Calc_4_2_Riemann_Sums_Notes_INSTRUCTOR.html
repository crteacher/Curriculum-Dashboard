Riemann Sums

Calculus I  ·  §4.2  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  INSTRUCTOR

We approximated integrals with a few rectangles. Now we formalize: partition the interval into $n$ equal pieces, add $n$ rectangles, and let $n\to\infty$. Which sum gives an overestimate? An underestimate?
Riemann Sum Setup

Partition $[a,b]$ into $n$ equal subintervals of width:

$$\Delta x=\frac{b-a}{n}$$

Left sum $L_n$: use left endpoint of each subinterval
$$L_n=\sum_{i=0}^{n-1}f(x_i)\,\Delta x$$

Right sum $R_n$: use right endpoint
$$R_n=\sum_{i=1}^{n}f(x_i)\,\Delta x$$

Midpoint sum: use center of each subinterval

Over/Underestimates & Sigma

For an increasing function on $[a,b]$:
Left sum → underestimate
Right sum → overestimate

For a decreasing function: opposite.

Sigma notation:
$\displaystyle\sum_{i=1}^{n}i=\dfrac{n(n+1)}{2}$
$\displaystyle\sum_{i=1}^{n}i^2=\dfrac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}$

As $n\to\infty$, all three sums converge to $\displaystyle\int_a^b f(x)\,dx$ for any integrable function. The more subintervals, the better the approximation.

Worked Examples
Example 1  —  Left Riemann sum
Approximate $\displaystyle\int_0^2 x^2\,dx$ using $n=4$ left rectangles.
$\Delta x=0.5$; left endpoints: $0, 0.5, 1, 1.5$
$L_4=0.5(0+0.25+1+2.25)=0.5\cdot 3.5=1.75$
Example 2  —  Right Riemann sum
Approximate $\displaystyle\int_0^2 x^2\,dx$ using $n=4$ right rectangles.
$\Delta x=0.5$; right endpoints: $0.5, 1, 1.5, 2$
$R_4=0.5(0.25+1+2.25+4)=0.5\cdot 7.5=3.75$
Example 3  —  Over/underestimate
For $f(x)=x^2$ on $[0,2]$, is $L_4$ an over- or underestimate?
$f(x)=x^2$ is increasing on $[0,2]$, so the left sum underestimates. ($L_4=1.75 < 8/3\approx 2.67$)
Example 4  —  Sigma notation
Write $R_n$ for $\displaystyle\int_0^1 x^2\,dx$ and evaluate the sum formula.
$\Delta x=1/n$, right endpoints $x_i=i/n$
$R_n=\sum_{i=1}^{n}\left(\dfrac{i}{n}\right)^2\cdot\dfrac{1}{n}=\dfrac{1}{n^3}\sum_{i=1}^{n}i^2=\dfrac{1}{n^3}\cdot\dfrac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}\to\dfrac{1}{3}$
Practice Problems
1.
Find $\Delta x$ for $\displaystyle\int_1^3 f(x)\,dx$ with $n=4$.
$\Delta x=(3-1)/4=0.5$
2.
Left sum for $\displaystyle\int_0^4 (x+1)\,dx$ with $n=4$.
$\Delta x=1$; left pts $0,1,2,3$; $L_4=1+2+3+4=10$
3.
Right sum for same integral.
Right pts $1,2,3,4$; $R_4=2+3+4+5=14$
4.
Is the left sum an over- or underestimate for $f(x)=x+1$ on $[0,4]$?
$f$ is increasing → left sum underestimates
5.
Evaluate $\displaystyle\sum_{i=1}^{4}(2i-1)$.
$1+3+5+7=16$
6.
What value does $R_n$ for $\displaystyle\int_0^1 x\,dx$ approach as $n\to\infty$?
$\frac{1}{2}$ (area of triangle with base 1, height 1)
