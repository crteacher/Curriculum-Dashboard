Squeeze Theorem & Intermediate Value Theorem

Calculus I  ·  §1.5  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  INSTRUCTOR

We know $-1\leq\sin(x)\leq 1$ for all $x$. So what can we say about $x^2\cdot\sin(x)$ near $x=0$? Can we find the limit without needing to evaluate $\sin(0)$ directly? And separately: can we prove an equation has a solution without actually solving it?
Squeeze Theorem

If $g(x)\leq f(x)\leq h(x)$ on an open interval around $a$, and $\displaystyle\lim_{x\to a}g(x)=\lim_{x\to a}h(x)=L$, then $\displaystyle\lim_{x\to a}f(x)=L$.

Strategy: bound $f(x)$ between two simpler functions that share the same limit, then squeeze.

Classic bounds:
$-1\leq\sin\theta\leq 1$   and   $-1\leq\cos\theta\leq 1$   for all $\theta$.
Multiply both sides by a non-negative factor to get your bounding functions.

Intermediate Value Theorem

If $f$ is continuous on $[a,b]$ and $N$ is strictly between $f(a)$ and $f(b)$, then $\exists\,c\in(a,b)$ with $f(c)=N$.

IVT template — proving $g(x)=0$ has a root:

  1. Rewrite as $g(x)=f(x)-k=0$
  2. Confirm $g$ is continuous on $[a,b]$
  3. Show $g(a)$ and $g(b)$ have opposite signs
  4. Conclude by IVT: $\exists\,c\in(a,b)$ with $g(c)=0$

IVT guarantees existence — it does NOT find the value.

Worked Examples
Example 1  —  Squeeze — $x^2\sin x$ at 0
Find $\displaystyle\lim_{x\to 0}x^2\sin x$.
Since $-1\leq\sin x\leq 1$:
$-x^2\leq x^2\sin x\leq x^2$
Both bounds $\to 0$ → limit $= 0$.
Example 2  —  Squeeze — $\sin(x)/x$ at infinity
Show $\displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}\dfrac{\sin x}{x}=0$.
$-1\leq\sin x\leq 1 \Rightarrow -\dfrac{1}{x}\leq\dfrac{\sin x}{x}\leq\dfrac{1}{x}$
Both bounds $\to 0$ as $x\to\infty$ → limit $= 0$.
Example 3  —  IVT — prove equation solvable
Show $3x^5-4x^2=3$ has a solution on $[0,2]$.
Let $g(x)=3x^5-4x^2-3$ (polynomial, continuous).
$g(0)=-3<0$   $g(2)=77>0$
Sign change → $\exists\,c\in(0,2)$ with $g(c)=0$.
Example 4  —  IVT — $\cos x = x$
Show $\cos x = x$ has a solution on $(0,2)$.
Let $g(x)=\cos x - x$ (continuous).
$g(0)=1>0$   $g(2)=\cos 2-2\approx -1.58<0$
Sign change → solution exists on $(0,2)$.
Practice Problems
1.
Find $\displaystyle\lim_{x\to 0}x^2\cos\!\left(\dfrac{1}{x}\right)$.
$0$   (squeeze: $-x^2\leq f\leq x^2$)
2.
Show $x^3+x-1=0$ has a root on $(0,1)$.
$g(0)=-1<0$, $g(1)=1>0$; continuous → IVT applies.
3.
Find $\displaystyle\lim_{x\to 0}x\sin\!\left(\dfrac{1}{x}\right)$.
$0$   (squeeze: $-|x|\leq f\leq |x|$)
4.
Show $e^x-x=2$ has a solution on $(0,4)$.
$h(x)=e^x-x-2$: $h(0)=-1<0$, $h(4)\approx 48.6>0$ → IVT.
5.
Find $\displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}\dfrac{\cos x}{x}$.
$0$   (squeeze: $-1/x\leq f\leq 1/x$)
6.
Show $x^5-2x=1$ has a root on $(1,2)$.
$g(1)=-2<0$, $g(2)=27>0$ → IVT.
