Displacement, Velocity & Acceleration

Calculus I  ·  §4.6  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  STUDENT

Derivatives connected position, velocity, and acceleration going downward. Antiderivatives let us go back up — from acceleration to velocity to position. And the definite integral of velocity gives displacement. But displacement and total distance are different — which is which?
The Position-Velocity-Acceleration Chain

Differentiate downward:
$s(t)\xrightarrow{\;d/dt\;}v(t)\xrightarrow{\;d/dt\;}a(t)$

Integrate upward:
$a(t)\xrightarrow{\;\int\;}v(t)+C_1\xrightarrow{\;\int\;}s(t)+C_2$

Use initial conditions $v(t_0)$ and $s(t_0)$ to find $C_1$ and $C_2$.

Displacement vs Total Distance

Displacement (signed):

$$\Delta s=\int_a^b v(t)\,dt=s(b)-s(a)$$

Can be negative if the object moves back.

Total distance (always $\geq 0$):

$$\text{distance}=\int_a^b |v(t)|\,dt$$

Split the integral at zeros of $v$ and add absolute values.

Object is moving right/up when $v>0$; left/down when $v<0$; stopped when $v=0$.

Displacement $= \int_a^b v(t)\,dt$ = net signed area (cancels). Total distance $= \int_a^b |v(t)|\,dt$ = sum of shaded areas.
Worked Examples
Example 1  —  Find velocity from acceleration
Acceleration $a(t)=6t$, $v(0)=2$. Find $v(t)$.
$v(t)=\int 6t\,dt=3t^2+C$
$v(0)=C=2 \Rightarrow v(t)=3t^2+2$
Example 2  —  Find position from velocity
$v(t)=3t^2+2$, $s(0)=1$. Find $s(t)$.
$s(t)=\int(3t^2+2)\,dt=t^3+2t+C$
$s(0)=C=1 \Rightarrow s(t)=t^3+2t+1$
Example 3  —  Displacement
$v(t)=t^2-4$ on $[0,3]$. Find displacement.
$\displaystyle\int_0^3(t^2-4)\,dt=\left[\dfrac{t^3}{3}-4t\right]_0^3=9-12=−3$ units
Example 4  —  Total distance
$v(t)=t^2-4$ on $[0,3]$. Find total distance traveled.
$v=0$ at $t=2$
$\int_0^2(4-t^2)\,dt+\int_2^3(t^2-4)\,dt$
$=\left[4t-t^3/3\right]_0^2+\left[t^3/3-4t\right]_2^3$
$=\dfrac{16}{3}+\dfrac{7}{3}=\dfrac{23}{3}$ units
Practice Problems
1.
$a(t)=4$, $v(0)=0$. Find $v(t)$.
$v(t)=4t$
2.
$v(t)=4t$, $s(0)=3$. Find $s(t)$.
$s(t)=2t^2+3$
3.
$v(t)=2t-6$ on $[0,4]$. Find displacement.
$\int_0^4(2t-6)\,dt=[t^2-6t]_0^4=16-24=-8$
4.
$v(t)=2t-6$ on $[0,4]$. Is the object moving right or left at $t=2$?
$v(2)=4-6=-2<0$ → moving left
5.
$s(t)=t^3-6t^2+9t$. When is the object stopped?
$v(t)=3t^2-12t+9=3(t-1)(t-3)=0$ at $t=1$ and $t=3$
6.
$v(t)=3t^2-12$. Find displacement on $[0,2]$.
$\int_0^2(3t^2-12)\,dt=[t^3-12t]_0^2=8-24=-16$
