Derivatives of sin, cos, eˣ, ln(x)

Calculus I  ·  §2.4  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  STUDENT

The Power Rule handles polynomial-type functions. But what about $\sin x$, $e^x$, and $\ln x$? These have their own derivative rules — and unlike the chain rule versions, these formulas apply only when the argument is plain $x$.
The Four Core Formulas

$$\frac{d}{dx}[\sin x]=\cos x \qquad \frac{d}{dx}[\cos x]=-\sin x$$

$$\frac{d}{dx}[e^x]=e^x \qquad \frac{d}{dx}[\ln x]=\frac{1}{x}$$

Combine with constant multiples and sums/differences just like the Power Rule.

Important: these formulas apply only when the argument is plain $x$ (not $2x$, $x^2$, etc. — that needs the Chain Rule).

Key Values to Know

$\sin(0)=0$   $\cos(0)=1$   $e^0=1$   $\ln(1)=0$

$\sin\!\left(\dfrac{\pi}{2}\right)=1$   $\cos\!\left(\dfrac{\pi}{2}\right)=0$

Use these to evaluate derivatives at $x=0$, $x=1$, $x=\pi/2$.

Memory trick for trig: derivative of sine is cosine (positive); derivative of cosine is negative sine. The negative sign moves from cosine to its derivative, not sine.

Worked Examples
Example 1  —  Mixed function
Differentiate $f(x)=3\sin x - 2e^x + 5x^2$.
Example 2  —  Tangent line
Find the tangent line to $f(x)=e^x$ at $x=0$.
Example 3  —  Trig tangent line
Find the tangent line to $f(x)=\sin x$ at $x=\pi/2$.
Example 4  —  Natural log derivative
Differentiate $g(x)=4\ln x - x^3 + \cos x$.
Practice Problems
1.
Differentiate $f(x)=5\cos x + 2x^3$.
2.
Differentiate $g(x)=e^x - \ln x + 7$.
3.
Find $f'(0)$ for $f(x)=\sin x + e^x$.
4.
Find the tangent line to $f(x)=\ln x$ at $x=1$.
5.
Find all $x\in[0,2\pi]$ where $f(x)=\sin x$ has horizontal tangents.
6.
Differentiate $h(x)=3\sin x - 4\cos x + e^x - 2\ln x$.
