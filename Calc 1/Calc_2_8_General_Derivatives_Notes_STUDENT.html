General Derivatives

Calculus I  ·  §2.8  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  STUDENT

We now have Product Rule, Quotient Rule, and Chain Rule. Real functions combine all three. How do you decide which rule to apply first, and how do you handle nested combinations?
Strategy: Read the Structure

Before differentiating, ask: what is the outermost operation?

  • Product at top level → Product Rule first
  • Quotient at top level → Quotient Rule first
  • Composite (one function inside another) → Chain Rule first

Then apply chain rule to any composite factors inside.

Work outermost to innermost, one layer at a time.

Key Values for Evaluation

Evaluating at $x=0$ or $x=1$ simplifies most mixed-rule expressions:

$e^0=1$   $\ln 1=0$   $\sin 0=0$   $\cos 0=1$

Example: for $f(x)=x^2 e^x$,
$f'(x)=2xe^x+x^2e^x$, so $f'(0)=0+0=0$.

Product Rule reminder: $(fg)'=f'g+fg'$
Quotient Rule reminder: $\left(\dfrac{f}{g}\right)'=\dfrac{f'g-fg'}{g^2}$
Don't forget to apply chain rule to composite pieces inside these.

Worked Examples
Example 1  —  Product + Chain
Differentiate $f(x)=x^2\sin(3x)$.
Example 2  —  Quotient + Chain
Differentiate $g(x)=\dfrac{e^{2x}}{x^2+1}$.
Example 3  —  Evaluate at a point
Let $f(x)=x\,e^x$. Find $f'(0)$.
Example 4  —  Chain inside product
Differentiate $h(x)=\sqrt{x}\cdot\ln(x^2)$.
Practice Problems

2.

3.

4.

5.

6.
