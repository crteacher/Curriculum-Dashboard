First Derivative Test

Calculus I  ·  §3.6  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  STUDENT

We found critical numbers — places where local extrema can occur. But not every critical number gives a max or min. How do we classify them? And how do we determine where a function is increasing versus decreasing?
Increasing & Decreasing

$f'(x)>0$ on an interval → $f$ is increasing there
$f'(x)<0$ on an interval → $f$ is decreasing there

Procedure:

  1. Find all critical numbers
  2. Build a sign chart: test $f'$ in each interval between critical numbers
  3. $+$ sign means increasing; $-$ sign means decreasing
First Derivative Test

At a critical number $x=c$:

  • $f'$ changes $+\to-$ → local max
  • $f'$ changes $-\to+$ → local min
  • No sign change → neither (inflection)

The sign of $f'$ determines the shape — up-then-down is a max, down-then-up is a min.

When testing the sign of $f'$ in an interval, pick any convenient test point inside it — you only need the sign, not the exact value.

Sign chart for $f'(x)=3x(x-2)$: $+\to-$ at $x=0$ (max), $-\to+$ at $x=2$ (min)
