Continuity

Calculus I  ·  §1.3  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026  ·  STUDENT

A function can have a limit at a point without actually equaling that value there. So what does it mean for a function to be continuous? And what does continuity guarantee about a function's behavior on an interval?
Continuity at a Point

$f$ is continuous at $c$ if all three hold:

  1. $f(c)$ is defined
  2. $\displaystyle\lim_{x\to c}f(x)$ exists
  3. $\displaystyle\lim_{x\to c}f(x) = f(c)$

Types of discontinuity:

  • Removable: limit exists but $\neq f(c)$ (or $f(c)$ undefined)
  • Jump: one-sided limits exist but differ
  • Infinite: limit is $\pm\infty$ (vertical asymptote)

Continuous everywhere: polynomials, $e^x$, $\sin x$, $\cos x$, and their combinations are continuous on all of $\mathbb{R}$. Rational functions are continuous wherever the denominator $\neq 0$.

Intermediate Value Theorem

If $f$ is continuous on $[a,b]$ and $N$ is any value strictly between $f(a)$ and $f(b)$, then there exists at least one $c\in(a,b)$ with $f(c)=N$.

IVT template (proving $g(x)=0$ has a root):

  1. Confirm $g$ is continuous on $[a,b]$
  2. Show $g(a)$ and $g(b)$ have opposite signs
  3. Conclude $\exists\,c\in(a,b)$ with $g(c)=0$

IVT guarantees existence, not uniqueness.

Three types of discontinuity: removable (hole), jump, infinite (vertical asymptote)
Worked Examples
Example 1  —  Checking continuity
Is $f(x)=\dfrac{x^2-1}{x-1}$ continuous at $x=1$?
Example 2  —  Finding $k$ for continuity
Find $k$ so $f(x)=\begin{cases}kx+1 & x\leq 2\\ x^2 & x>2\end{cases}$ is continuous.
Example 3  —  IVT — proving a root exists
Does $x^3+x-1=0$ have a root on $(0,1)$?
Example 4  —  Classifying a discontinuity
Classify the discontinuity of $f(x)=\dfrac{x^2-4}{x-2}$ at $x=2$.
Practice Problems
1.
Is $f(x)=\dfrac{1}{x-1}$ continuous at $x=2$?

Is $f(x)=\dfrac{x^2-1}{x-1}$ continuous at $x=1$?
3.

4.
Where is $f(x)=\dfrac{1}{x^2-4}$ discontinuous?
5.
Does IVT guarantee a root of $x^3-5x+1=0$ on $(0,1)$?
6.
