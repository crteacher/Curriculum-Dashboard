Power Series

Calculus 2  ·  §28  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Radius of Convergence For $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} c_n(x-a)^n$, the Ratio Test gives $\displaystyle\frac{1}{R} = \lim_{n\to\infty}\left|\frac{c_{n+1}}{c_n}\right|$. The series converges absolutely for $|x-a| < R$, diverges for $|x-a| > R$. Special cases: $R=0$ (converges only at $a$), $R=\infty$ (converges everywhere).
Interval of Convergence The interval of convergence is found by solving $|x-a| < R$, then testing each endpoint separately with another convergence test. Endpoints may converge, diverge, or one of each — they must each be checked.
Geometric Power Series $\displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} x^n = \frac{1}{1-x}$ for $|x| < 1$. Substituting $-x$, $x^2$, or $cx$ generates new power series; term-by-term differentiation and integration preserve the radius of convergence.
Practice Problems

Enter integers or fractions like 3/2 for radius questions. Use inf for $R=\infty$. For intervals use notation like [-1,1).
