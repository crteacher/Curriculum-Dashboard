Surface Area of Revolution

Calculus 2  ·  §10  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Surface Area — Rotation about x-axis $\displaystyle S=2\pi\int_a^b f(x)\sqrt{1+[f'(x)]^2}\,dx$. Each arc element $ds$ sweeps a band of circumference $2\pi f(x)$; summing those bands gives total surface area.
Surface Area — Rotation about y-axis $\displaystyle S=2\pi\int_a^b x\sqrt{1+[f'(x)]^2}\,dx$. The radius of each band is now $x$ (distance from the $y$-axis), not $f(x)$.
Connection to Arc Length & Sanity Checks Surface area = arc length integral with an extra $2\pi r$ factor. Check: rotating $y=rx/h$ on $[0,h]$ about the $x$-axis gives $\pi r\ell$ (cone lateral area), where $\ell=\sqrt{r^2+h^2}$.
