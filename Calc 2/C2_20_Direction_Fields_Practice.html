Direction Fields & Numerical Methods

Calculus 2  ·  §20  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Equilibrium Solutions An equilibrium solution is a constant solution $y=c$ where $dy/dx=0$ for all $x$. Set $f(x,y)=0$ and solve for $y$. Equilibria divide the direction field into regions where solutions are increasing or decreasing.
Stability A stable equilibrium attracts nearby solutions — if $y$ starts close to the equilibrium, it moves toward it. An unstable equilibrium repels nearby solutions. Check the sign of $dy/dx$ just above and below the equilibrium.
Euler's Method Given $dy/dx = f(x,y)$ with $y(x_0)=y_0$, each step predicts $y_{n+1} = y_n + h\cdot f(x_n,y_n)$, where $h$ is the step size. Euler's method approximates the solution by following the slope at each point.
