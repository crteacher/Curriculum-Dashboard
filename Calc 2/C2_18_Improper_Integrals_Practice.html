Improper Integrals

Calculus 2  ·  §18  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Type I — Infinite Limits$\displaystyle\int_a^\infty f(x)\,dx=\lim_{t\to\infty}\int_a^t f(x)\,dx$. The integral converges if this limit is finite; otherwise it diverges. Split $\int_{-\infty}^\infty$ at any convenient point.
Type II — Discontinuous IntegrandsIf $f$ blows up at $c\in[a,b]$, replace with a limit approaching $c$. Example: $\int_0^1 x^{-1/2}\,dx=\lim_{t\to 0^+}\int_t^1 x^{-1/2}\,dx=2$.
p-Integral Benchmark$\displaystyle\int_1^\infty x^{-p}\,dx$ converges iff $p>1$, giving value $\tfrac{1}{p-1}$. This benchmark quickly settles many convergence questions.
