Rotational Solids: Disk & Washer

Calculus 2  ·  §7  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Disk MethodRotate $y=f(x)\geq 0$ about the $x$-axis on $[a,b]$: $\displaystyle V=\pi\int_a^b[f(x)]^2\,dx$. Each slice is a disk with radius $f(x)$.
Washer MethodRotate the region between $f$ (outer) and $g$ (inner): $\displaystyle V=\pi\int_a^b\bigl([f]^2-[g]^2\bigr)\,dx$. Subtract inner radius squared from outer.
Other AxesRotating about $y=k$: radius becomes $|f(x)-k|$. Rotating about the $y$-axis: express $x$ as a function of $y$ and integrate in $y$.
