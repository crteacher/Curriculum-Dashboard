Partial Fraction Decomposition

Calculus 2  ·  §16  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Setup: Factor FirstFactor the denominator completely before decomposing. If $\deg p\geq\deg q$, do polynomial long division first. Each distinct linear factor $(x-a)$ gets $\dfrac{A}{x-a}$; a repeated factor $(x-a)^2$ gets $\dfrac{A}{x-a}+\dfrac{B}{(x-a)^2}$.
Solving for CoefficientsMultiply through by the denominator to get a polynomial identity. Plug in the roots of each factor to isolate coefficients quickly — or equate coefficients of like powers.
Integrating the Pieces$\displaystyle\int\frac{A}{x-a}\,dx=A\ln|x-a|+C$. Repeated linear factors integrate by the power rule. The decomposition converts one hard integral into a sum of simple ones.
