Alternating Series

Calculus 2  ·  §26  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Alternating Series Test (AST) $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}(-1)^{n-1}b_n$ converges if (1) $b_n > 0$, (2) $b_{n+1} \leq b_n$ (decreasing), and (3) $\displaystyle\lim_{n\to\infty}b_n = 0$.
Alternating Series Estimation If the AST conditions hold, the error in the $N$th partial sum satisfies $|S - S_N| \leq b_{N+1}$. The error is bounded by the first omitted term.
Absolute vs. Conditional Convergence A series is absolutely convergent if $\sum|a_n|$ converges; conditionally convergent if $\sum a_n$ converges but $\sum|a_n|$ diverges. The alternating harmonic series is a classic conditional example.

Type convergent or divergent for convergence questions. For fractions use p/q.
