Divergence & Integral Tests

Calculus 2  ·  §24  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Divergence Test If $\displaystyle\lim_{n\to\infty} a_n \neq 0$, then $\sum a_n$ diverges. If the limit equals 0, the test is inconclusive — the series may converge or diverge.
Integral Test If $f$ is positive, continuous, and decreasing on $[1,\infty)$ with $f(n)=a_n$, then $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n$ and $\displaystyle\int_1^{\infty} f(x)\,dx$ both converge or both diverge.
$p$-Series $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^p}$ converges if $p > 1$ and diverges if $p \leq 1$. The harmonic series $\sum 1/n$ ($p=1$) diverges.
