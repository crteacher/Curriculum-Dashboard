Core Ideas
Work against a Varying Force $W = \displaystyle\int_a^b F(x)\,dx$. Hooke's Law: $F=kx$ (spring constant $k$, displacement $x$ from natural length). Find $k$ from given data, then integrate over the displacement interval.
Lifting & Cable Problems A chain/cable of weight density $w$ N/m being raised: at position $x$, the hanging length remaining is $L-x$, so $F=w(L-x)$. Total work: $W=\displaystyle\int_0^L w(L-x)\,dx = \frac{wL^2}{2}$. For lifting a solid object: $W = F\cdot d$ (constant force times distance).
Pumping Liquids & Variable-Density Rods Pump water from a tank: slice at height $y$, weight of slice $= \rho g A(y)\,dy$, lifted distance $= h-y$. Rod mass: $m=\displaystyle\int_a^b \rho(x)\,dx$ where $\rho(x)$ is linear density (kg/m).
