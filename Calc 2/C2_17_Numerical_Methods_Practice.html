Numerical Methods

Calculus 2  ·  §17  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Midpoint & Trapezoidal RulesWith $n$ subintervals, $\Delta x=(b-a)/n$. $M_n=\Delta x[f(\bar x_1)+\cdots+f(\bar x_n)]$ (midpoints). $T_n=\tfrac{\Delta x}{2}[f(x_0)+2f(x_1)+\cdots+2f(x_{n-1})+f(x_n)]$.
Simpson's RuleRequires $n$ even: $S_n=\tfrac{\Delta x}{3}[f(x_0)+4f(x_1)+2f(x_2)+\cdots+4f(x_{n-1})+f(x_n)]$. Coefficients: $1,4,2,4,\ldots,4,1$. Uses parabolic arcs; exact for polynomials of degree $\leq 3$.
AccuracyFor smooth functions: $S_n$ is more accurate than $T_n$, both better than $M_n$ for the same $n$. Error: $|E_T|\leq K_2(b-a)^3/(12n^2)$, $|E_S|\leq K_4(b-a)^5/(180n^4)$.
