Trig Integrals & Power Reduction

Calculus 2  ·  §3  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Odd Power Strategy For $\int \sin^m x\cos^n x\,dx$ where one power is odd, factor out one copy of that function. Use $\sin^2 x = 1-\cos^2 x$ (or $\cos^2 x = 1-\sin^2 x$) to replace the remaining even power, then let $u$ equal the other function.
E.g. $\cos^3 x = (1-\sin^2 x)\cos x$ → set $u = \sin x$.
Power-Reduction Identities (Even Powers) When both powers are even, reduce using: $$\sin^2 x = \frac{1-\cos(2x)}{2} \qquad \cos^2 x = \frac{1+\cos(2x)}{2}$$ Apply repeatedly until only first-power trig functions remain.
Double-Angle Product The identity $\sin x\cos x = \tfrac{1}{2}\sin(2x)$ is often the fastest route for even-power products: $\sin^2 x\cos^2 x = \tfrac{1}{4}\sin^2(2x)$.
