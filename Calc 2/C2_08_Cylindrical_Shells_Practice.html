Cylindrical Shells

Calculus 2  ·  §8  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Shell FormulaRotating the region under $y=f(x)\geq 0$ on $[a,b]$ about the $y$-axis: $\displaystyle V=2\pi\int_a^b x\,f(x)\,dx$. Each vertical strip at $x$ unrolls into a shell with radius $x$, height $f(x)$, thickness $dx$.
When to Use ShellsShells are easiest when the axis of rotation is parallel to the slicing direction. Rotating about the $y$-axis while integrating in $x$ gives shells naturally, avoiding the need to invert $f$.
Other AxesTo rotate about $x=k$: shell radius becomes $|x-k|$. To rotate about the $x$-axis using shells: $V=2\pi\int_c^d y\,g(y)\,dy$.
