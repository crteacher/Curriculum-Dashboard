Volumes of Solids

Calculus 2  ·  §6  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Slicing Method$\displaystyle V=\int_a^b A(x)\,dx$ where $A(x)$ is the cross-sectional area at position $x$. Works for any shape cross-section.
Disk MethodRotate $y=f(x)\geq 0$ about the $x$-axis: $\displaystyle V=\pi\int_a^b[f(x)]^2\,dx$. Each slice is a disk with radius $f(x)$.
Washer MethodRegion between $f(x)$ (outer) and $g(x)$ (inner) rotated about $x$-axis: $\displaystyle V=\pi\int_a^b\bigl([f]^2-[g]^2\bigr)\,dx$.
