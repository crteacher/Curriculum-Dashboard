Series

Calculus 2  ·  §23  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Partial Sums The $n$th partial sum of a series is $S_n = a_1 + a_2 + \cdots + a_n$. The series converges if $\displaystyle\lim_{n\to\infty} S_n$ exists.
Geometric Series $\displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} ar^n = \frac{a}{1-r}$ when $|r| < 1$. If $|r| \geq 1$, the series diverges. The partial sum formula is $S_n = a\,\dfrac{1-r^n}{1-r}$.
Divergence Test If $\displaystyle\lim_{n\to\infty} a_n \neq 0$, the series $\sum a_n$ diverges. This test can only prove divergence — passing it does not prove convergence.
