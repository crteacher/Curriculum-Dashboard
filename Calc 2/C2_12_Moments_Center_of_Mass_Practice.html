Moments & Center of Mass

Calculus 2  ·  §12  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Centroid Formulas For the region under $y=f(x)$ on $[a,b]$ with uniform density, the centroid $(\bar{x},\bar{y})$ satisfies: $\displaystyle\bar{x} = \frac{\int_a^b x\,f(x)\,dx}{\int_a^b f(x)\,dx}$, $\quad\displaystyle\bar{y} = \frac{\tfrac{1}{2}\int_a^b [f(x)]^2\,dx}{\int_a^b f(x)\,dx}$.
Moments $M_y = \rho\!\int_a^b x\,f(x)\,dx$ (moment about $y$-axis, depends on horizontal distance). $M_x = \rho\!\int_a^b \tfrac{1}{2}[f(x)]^2\,dx$ (moment about $x$-axis, average height $\tfrac{f(x)}{2}$ times mass). Density $\rho$ cancels when computing centroids.
Interpretation The centroid is the balance point of the region. For symmetric regions, $\bar{x}$ is the midpoint. For a rectangle of width $b$ and height $h$: $\bar{x}=b/2$, $\bar{y}=h/2$.
