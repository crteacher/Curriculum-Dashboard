Sequences

Calculus 2  ·  §22  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Arithmetic & Geometric Sequences Arithmetic: $a_n = a_1 + (n-1)d$. Geometric: $a_n = a_1 \cdot r^{n-1}$. The $n$th term is determined by the first term and the common difference $d$ or ratio $r$.
Limits of Sequences $\displaystyle\lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{p(n)}{q(n)}$: divide numerator and denominator by the highest power in the denominator. If $|r|<1$, then $r^n\to 0$.
Convergence, Monotone, Bounded A sequence converges if its limit exists and is finite; otherwise it diverges. A monotone bounded sequence always converges.
