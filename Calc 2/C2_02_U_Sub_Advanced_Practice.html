U-Substitution: Advanced Techniques

Calculus 2  ·  §2  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
The x = u − c Trick When $u = x + c$, you can write $x = u - c$. This lets you handle integrands like $\displaystyle\int x\sqrt{x+c}\,dx$ by expressing everything in terms of $u$. Set $u = x+c$, so $x = u-c$ and $dx = du$, then expand and integrate.
Logarithmic U-Sub If the numerator is (or is a multiple of) the derivative of the denominator: $$\int \frac{f'(x)}{f(x)}\,dx = \ln|f(x)| + C$$ Set $u = f(x)$, $du = f'(x)\,dx$, and the integral becomes $\int \frac{du}{u} = \ln|u|+C$.
When the Numerator Has Higher Degree If the degree of the numerator $\ge$ degree of the denominator, perform polynomial long division first to rewrite the integrand, then apply u-sub to any remaining fraction.
