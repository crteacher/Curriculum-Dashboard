Taylor Series

Calculus 2  ·  §29  ·  Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Taylor / Maclaurin Series If $f$ has derivatives of all orders at $a$: $\displaystyle f(x)=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\frac{f^{(n)}(a)}{n!}(x-a)^n$. When $a=0$ this is the Maclaurin series. The coefficient of $(x-a)^n$ is always $c_n = f^{(n)}(a)/n!$.
Key Standard Series $e^x = \displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\frac{x^n}{n!}$, $\quad\sin x = \displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^n x^{2n+1}}{(2n+1)!}$, $\quad\cos x = \displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^n x^{2n}}{(2n)!}$. All three converge for all real $x$.
ln and Geometric $\ln(1+x) = \displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^n x^{n+1}}{n+1} = x - \frac{x^2}{2}+\frac{x^3}{3}-\cdots$, valid for $-1 < x \leq 1$. The geometric series $\frac{1}{1-x}=\sum x^n$ serves as the foundation for many others.
Practice Problems

Enter coefficients as fractions like 1/6 or -1/2. Use inf for infinite radius.
